# winded.vertigo /what page — image generation prompts

*prompts to visualise the feeling, philosophy, and energy of "learning is change."*

these prompts are designed to capture the specific emotional and conceptual territory of the /what page copy — vertigo as growth signal, breathless excitement at the precipice, playful learning as transformative infrastructure, the interconnectedness of everything.

---

## series 1: "the precipice" — vertigo as invitation

these capture the core metaphor: approaching the edge of uncertainty not with fear but with breathless excitement.

### 1A — the edge (hero image)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A person standing at the very edge of an impossibly tall cliff, seen from behind and slightly above. The cliff edge is crisp and geometric — almost like the edge of a folded piece of paper — while the space beyond is vast, luminous, and alive with swirling colour currents (dusty blues, warm ambers, soft terracottas) that suggest wind, weather, possibility.
>
> The person's posture is not fearful. They lean slightly forward, weight on the balls of their feet, arms relaxed at their sides, chin slightly lifted. Their hair and clothing move gently in an updraft. The body language reads as anticipation, not anxiety. Breathless excitement, not hesitation.
>
> The cliff surface has subtle crease-line patterns — like origami fold marks — suggesting this ground was made, not found. Below and beyond, the swirling colours contain faint geometric structures: hints of networks, connections, pathways half-forming and dissolving.
>
> The light source is ahead and slightly below — the glow comes from the unknown, not from behind. The person is lit by what they're moving toward.
>
> Warm palette. No harsh contrasts. The feeling is sublime in the original sense — awe-inspiring scale that invites rather than overwhelms.
>
> Aspect ratio 16:9. Style: contemporary editorial illustration, painterly but precise, atmospheric.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> person standing at edge of impossibly tall geometric cliff, seen from behind. cliff surface has origami crease-line patterns. vast luminous space beyond filled with swirling colour currents in dusty blue, amber, terracotta — suggesting wind and possibility. person leans forward with anticipation not fear, hair moving in updraft. light comes from ahead, from the unknown. faint geometric networks visible in the swirling colours below. warm palette, sublime scale that invites not overwhelms. contemporary editorial illustration, painterly and atmospheric. 16:9.

### 1B — the lean (intimate, close-up)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> An extreme close-up of bare feet at the edge of a cliff or ledge. The toes curl slightly over the edge — not gripping in fear, but feeling the boundary. The surface beneath the feet has the texture of thick handmade paper with visible crease lines radiating outward from the edge.
>
> Beyond the edge: open air filled with tiny floating particles — seeds, paper fragments, firefly-like specks of light — drifting upward in a warm thermal current. The depth below is suggested but not shown directly; instead, the space is filled with soft gradients of blue and amber light.
>
> One foot is planted. The other is mid-step, hovering just past the edge — the moment before the leap, or the playful test of what "edge" means.
>
> Warm, intimate light. Shallow depth of field — the feet are sharp, the space beyond is soft and luminous. The feeling is tender, brave, and curious.
>
> Aspect ratio 4:5 (portrait). Style: photorealistic with painterly atmospheric effects.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> extreme close-up of bare feet at edge of paper-textured cliff, toes curling over the edge. crease lines radiate from the edge. beyond: open air with floating particles, seeds, paper fragments, firefly specks drifting upward. soft gradients of blue and amber light in the depth. one foot planted, one hovering past the edge mid-step. warm intimate light, shallow depth of field. tender brave curious feeling. photorealistic with painterly atmosphere. 4:5 portrait.

### 1C — the updraft (abstract/kinetic)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> An abstract vertical composition. A powerful upward current of air rendered as flowing lines, paper fragments, seeds, and light particles all rising through the frame from bottom to top. The movement is joyful, not chaotic — like a murmuration of starlings or confetti in a warm updraft.
>
> Within the rising current, folded paper forms at various stages of transformation: tight paper airplanes near the bottom, partially unfolded sheets in the middle, fully open pages with visible crease patterns near the top — the fold/unfold journey happening in the air itself.
>
> The colour shifts from warm earthy tones at the bottom (grounding, terracotta, brown paper) through vibrant blues and ambers in the middle (energy, play) to luminous white-gold at the top (aliveness, possibility).
>
> No ground. No sky. Just the movement itself — vertigo as a vertical sensation of being carried upward by something bigger than yourself.
>
> Aspect ratio 9:16 (tall portrait). Style: dynamic editorial illustration, kinetic and luminous.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> abstract vertical composition, powerful upward current of air rendered as flowing lines, paper fragments, seeds, and light particles all rising through the frame. joyful not chaotic, like a murmuration. folded paper forms at various stages: tight paper airplanes near bottom, partially unfolded sheets in middle, fully open crease-patterned pages near top. colour shifts from earthy terracotta at bottom through vibrant blue and amber in middle to luminous white-gold at top. no ground no sky, just upward movement. vertigo as being carried by something bigger. dynamic kinetic editorial illustration. 9:16 tall portrait.

## series 2: "interconnectedness of everything" — the learning ecosystem

these capture the ecological, networked, living-system quality of the work.

### 2A — the mycelium map (hero image)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A bird's-eye view of a vast, living network that looks simultaneously like mycelium spreading through soil, neural pathways firing, and a city seen from space at night. The network is rendered on what appears to be a large sheet of handmade paper — the fibres of the paper itself becoming the network.
>
> Nodes glow at different intensities — some bright amber (active, growing), some steady blue (established, stable), some flickering terracotta (emerging, new). The connections between nodes are not straight lines but organic, branching, sometimes looping back on themselves.
>
> At various points in the network, small scenes are visible as if embedded in the paper: a circle of people playing, a hand turning a page, a child laughing, a crease being made in paper, a seed germinating. These are tiny — almost like marginalia in a medieval manuscript — but alive.
>
> The edges of the paper/network fade into darkness, but tendrils continue beyond the frame, suggesting the network extends further than what we can see.
>
> The feeling is of looking at something ancient and alive — a system that has been growing for a long time and will continue to grow.
>
> Aspect ratio 1:1. Style: detailed editorial illustration, biological-meets-informational, warm and intricate.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> bird's eye view of a vast living network resembling mycelium, neural pathways, and city lights simultaneously. rendered on handmade paper whose fibres become the network itself. nodes glow at different intensities — amber for active, blue for established, terracotta for emerging. organic branching connections loop and curve. tiny embedded scenes at nodes: people playing, hands folding paper, seeds germinating — like living marginalia. edges fade to darkness but tendrils continue beyond frame. ancient and alive, a growing system. detailed editorial illustration, biological meets informational, warm and intricate. 1:1 square.

### 2B — the ecosystem cross-section (educational, detailed)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A cross-section illustration — like a scientific diagram of a forest floor or coral reef — showing multiple layers of a learning ecosystem coexisting vertically.
>
> Bottom layer (roots/soil): dense, dark, textured like paper pulp. Crease lines run through it like root systems. Small text fragments are embedded in the soil — words like "assumptions," "habits," "defaults" — the hidden structures.
>
> Middle layer (surface/canopy): a vibrant, diverse landscape of activity. Small illustrated figures are engaged in different forms of play and learning — folding paper, building structures, having conversations, drawing, moving. The figures are diverse in form and scale. Some are human, some are more abstract — suggesting the "more-than-human" dimension.
>
> Top layer (atmosphere/spore): ideas, insights, and discoveries floating upward as luminous particles, paper fragments with crease patterns, seeds, and small geometric forms. These are the outputs — what the ecosystem produces — drifting outward to land elsewhere and seed new growth.
>
> The layers are connected by vertical flows: roots feed the surface, surface activity releases the atmospheric layer, atmosphere feeds back down as rain/nutrients. A visible cycle.
>
> Aspect ratio 9:16 (tall portrait). Style: scientific illustration meets children's book illustration — detailed, warm, inviting.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> cross-section illustration like a scientific diagram of a forest floor. three vertical layers. bottom: dark paper-pulp soil with crease-line root systems and embedded text fragments ("assumptions" "habits" "defaults"). middle: vibrant landscape with small diverse figures engaged in play, folding paper, building, conversing, drawing. some human some abstract. top: luminous particles, paper fragments, seeds floating upward — ideas and discoveries drifting outward. vertical flows connect all layers in a visible cycle. scientific illustration meets children's book warmth, detailed and inviting. 9:16 tall portrait.

### 2C — the seed moment (intimate, human)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> Two pairs of hands — different skin tones, different sizes (suggesting adult and child, or two collaborators) — cupped together around a small glowing object. The object is ambiguous: it could be a seed, a crumpled ball of paper beginning to unfold, a small light, or all three at once.
>
> The light from the object illuminates the hands from within, casting warm amber light on the skin. Fine crease lines are visible on the paper-seed, suggesting it has been folded and is now opening.
>
> The background is dark and soft — the focus is entirely on this small shared moment. A few tiny particles of light float upward from the opening, like spores or sparks.
>
> The image captures the /what page line: "every interaction seeds opportunity for growth, connections, and lifelong learning." This is that seed. This is that interaction.
>
> Aspect ratio 1:1. Style: warm photorealistic, chiaroscuro lighting, intimate.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> two pairs of hands — different skin tones, different sizes — cupped together around a small glowing ambiguous object that is simultaneously a seed, an unfolding paper ball, and a small light. warm amber glow illuminates hands from within. fine crease lines visible on the paper-seed as it opens. dark soft background, tiny particles of light floating upward like spores. intimate shared moment. warm photorealistic, chiaroscuro lighting. 1:1 square.

## series 3: "playful leaps" — the energy and joy of the work

these capture the kinetic, joyful, slightly wild energy of "dodging obstacles and conventions."

### 3A — the leap (full body, dynamic)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A figure mid-leap between two floating paper platforms — large origami-like geometric planes that hover in open space at different heights and angles. The figure is caught at the peak of the jump: body extended, relaxed, joyful. Not athletic — playful. The posture suggests someone who jumped not because they had to but because they wanted to see what would happen.
>
> The paper platforms have visible crease lines and fold marks. Some platforms are partially folded — rising at angles, creating ramps and edges. Others are flat and stable. The arrangement suggests a landscape you have to navigate by leaping, climbing, sliding — an obstacle course made of ideas.
>
> Between the platforms, the space is filled with warm light and floating paper fragments — the debris of conventions being discarded, obstacles being dodged. Some fragments still have text on them: "standardised," "compliance," "metrics," "one-size-fits-all" — the old paradigm, dissolving.
>
> The palette is warm and alive. The lighting is theatrical — a single warm source creating long shadows and bright highlights on the paper surfaces. The overall feeling is exhilarating and free.
>
> Aspect ratio 16:9. Style: editorial illustration, dynamic composition, warm cinematic lighting.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> figure mid-leap between floating origami-like geometric paper platforms at different heights and angles in open space. body extended, relaxed, joyful — playful not athletic. paper platforms have visible crease lines and fold marks, some partially folded creating ramps and edges. floating paper fragments between platforms with fading text: "standardised" "compliance" "metrics" — old paradigm dissolving. warm alive palette, theatrical lighting with long shadows. exhilarating and free feeling. editorial illustration, dynamic composition, cinematic lighting. 16:9.

---

### 3B — the paper airplane that isn't (brand icon moment)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A single paper airplane in flight — but something is wrong with it, beautifully wrong. It is mid-transformation: the nose is still pointed forward in flight, but the tail and wings are unfolding, opening, becoming something else. Paper is peeling back to reveal crease-line patterns underneath. The airplane is simultaneously flying and disassembling — not crashing, but evolving.
>
> It trails a wake of small luminous particles and tiny paper fragments — not destruction debris but transformation residue. Some of the trailing fragments are beginning to fold into new, different shapes: a flower, a hand, a star, a spiral.
>
> The background is a clean, warm gradient — deep blue at the top fading to amber at the bottom, like a sky at the moment between sunset and night. The airplane moves from left to right, slightly upward.
>
> This single image captures the entire /what page philosophy: something that starts as a familiar, expected form (a paper airplane) refuses to stay that way. It unfolds into something wilder, more alive, and more true.
>
> Aspect ratio 16:9. Style: clean editorial illustration, precise linework with atmospheric colour, slightly surreal.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> a single paper airplane in flight mid-transformation — nose still pointed forward but tail and wings unfolding, opening, becoming something else. paper peeling back to reveal crease-line patterns. simultaneously flying and evolving, not crashing. trailing luminous particles and tiny paper fragments that are folding into new shapes: a flower, a hand, a star, a spiral. warm gradient background from deep blue top to amber bottom. airplane moves left to right, slightly upward. clean editorial illustration, precise linework with atmospheric colour, slightly surreal. 16:9.

### 3C — the dizzy (abstract, sensory)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> An abstract image that captures the physical sensation of vertigo — but rendered as joyful, not disorienting. The composition spirals gently around a luminous centre point. Concentric rings of colour and texture rotate at slightly different speeds: paper textures, crease-line patterns, watercolour washes, typographic fragments, tiny illustrated figures, botanical drawings, geometric forms.
>
> The spiral is not tight and anxious — it is wide, breathing, like a slow whirlpool or galaxy. The elements within it are diverse and playful: a paper airplane, a child's hand, a crease pattern, a seed pod, a neural network node, a laughing face, a question mark, musical notation. The interconnectedness of everything, spinning gently.
>
> At the very centre: a small clear space — an eye of the storm — that is pure warm white light. Stillness inside the motion. This is the "aliveness" at the heart of the vertigo.
>
> The colour palette spirals too: terracotta and brown at the outer edges (the earthy, grounded), blues and ambers in the middle rings (the active, playful), white-gold at the centre (the alive, the possible).
>
> The overall feeling is of being happily, willingly dizzy — "we love that feeling of vertigo."
>
> Aspect ratio 1:1. Style: mixed-media collage illustration, layered textures, warm and kaleidoscopic.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> abstract spiral composition capturing joyful vertigo. concentric rings rotating gently around a luminous centre — rings made of paper textures, crease-line patterns, watercolour washes, tiny illustrated figures, botanical drawings, geometric forms. wide breathing spiral not tight or anxious. diverse elements within: paper airplane, child's hand, seed pod, neural network node, laughing face, question mark. at the centre: small clear space of pure warm white light — stillness inside motion. colour spirals from earthy terracotta at edges through blue and amber to white-gold centre. happily willingly dizzy. mixed-media collage illustration, layered textures, warm and kaleidoscopic. 1:1 square.

## series 4: "making a yes easier" — the practical, partner-facing dimension

these capture the part of the copy about preparing partners to gain stakeholder support.

### 4A — the bridge (strategic, clear)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A conceptual illustration showing two cliff edges facing each other across a gap. The left cliff is rigid, angular, grey — representing the institutional world (a few small icons suggest: spreadsheets, org charts, approval stamps, policy documents). The right cliff is organic, colourful, alive — representing the learning world (small icons: playing hands, growing plants, folding paper, laughing faces).
>
> Spanning the gap: a bridge made of paper. Not flimsy — architecturally beautiful, like an origami suspension bridge with visible crease-line engineering. The bridge is strong precisely because of how it's been folded. Light passes through the paper at the fold points, creating a warm glow along the structure.
>
> A small figure walks across the bridge from right to left, carrying something luminous — bringing the evidence, the story, the compelling case from the world of play into the world of policy.
>
> Below the bridge, the gap is not empty but filled with soft light and floating fragments — seeds of connection already drifting between the two worlds.
>
> The message: we build the bridge between transformative learning and institutional approval. We make the yes easier.
>
> Aspect ratio 21:9 (ultrawide). Style: editorial illustration, conceptual and clean, warm colours.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> conceptual illustration of two cliff edges facing each other across a gap. left cliff is rigid angular grey with institutional icons (spreadsheets, org charts, approval stamps). right cliff is organic colourful alive with learning icons (playing hands, growing plants, folding paper). spanning the gap: a beautiful origami paper bridge with visible crease-line engineering, light glowing through fold points. small figure walks across carrying something luminous from right to left. gap below filled with soft light and floating seed fragments. editorial illustration, conceptual and clean, warm colours. ultrawide 21:9.

### 4B — the evidence garden (poetic data)

**DALL-E prompt:**

> A garden — but instead of flowers, the plants growing from rich dark soil are data visualisations, charts, and story fragments. Bar charts grow like stems. Scatter plots bloom like flower heads. Narrative text wraps around branches like vines. A qualitative quote hangs from a leaf like a dewdrop.
>
> The garden is tended by small illustrated figures — some pruning, some planting, some harvesting, some simply sitting and looking. The figures work with care: this is not data extraction, it is data cultivation.
>
> The soil itself is made of paper — layers of crease-patterned sheets composted into fertile ground. You can see fragments of old fold patterns in the earth, suggesting that evidence grows from the creases of experience.
>
> Above the garden, some of the data-plants release seeds — small luminous icons that float upward and outward: a green checkmark, a handshake, a stamp of approval, a smiling face. These are the "yeses" — the stakeholder support that grows from well-tended evidence.
>
> Warm, golden-hour light. The feeling is of patient, beautiful, rigorous work that produces real results.
>
> Aspect ratio 16:9. Style: whimsical editorial illustration, detailed and warm, magical realism.

**Nano Banana prompt:**

> a garden where the plants are data visualisations — bar charts grow like stems, scatter plots bloom like flowers, narrative text wraps like vines, a quote hangs from a leaf like a dewdrop. small illustrated figures tend the garden: pruning, planting, harvesting, sitting and looking. soil is made of layered crease-patterned paper composted into fertile ground. above the garden, data-plants release seed icons floating upward: green checkmarks, handshakes, approval stamps — the yeses that grow from evidence. warm golden-hour light. patient beautiful rigorous work. whimsical editorial illustration, detailed and warm, magical realism. 16:9.

## recommended hero image for the /what page

**3B — the paper airplane that isn't.** It captures the entire philosophy in a single visual: something familiar refusing to stay familiar, unfolding into something wilder, more alive, and more true. It also connects directly to the physical mailer, the creaseworks activity, and the Find/Fold/Unfold/Find Again process — making it a visual bridge between the /what page and the rest of the brand.
