      before the day

      every attendee receives a question to sit with, a note about the day's design, and a name badge with a QR code linking to their personal conference page.

      this is not a standard conference. there are moments that ask something of you. all of them are optional. none of them are difficult.

      OpenEd is a one-day conference for educators and edtech practitioners. what follows shows how the format of a conference could be redesigned to embody the same principles it is arguing for.

      the session where you prototype something that might not work. failure is data. the brief is the same for everyone. the entry point is not.

      MAIN HALL

      does open education scale?

      four practitioners debate. no consensus required.

      the panel does not begin with answers. it begins with the hardest question in the room. what happens to open pedagogy when you have 500 learners and one facilitator?

      QUIET ROOM

      the quiet room

      reflection cards. soft music. no agenda.

      some people need noise and conversation to process. some need stillness. both are legitimate. needing quiet is not opting out. it is a different way of being in.

      WORKSHOP

      open by design

      build a lesson unit with open tools in 45 minutes. bring a real context. leave with something you can use.

      a short paired exercise. map where you are.

      the template is there for people who think better with a structure. the conversation is for people who think better out loud.

      you leave with something named — a practice, a gap, a person you need to find.
      ten minutes. no debrief required.
