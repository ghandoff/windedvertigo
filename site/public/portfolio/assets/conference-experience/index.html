    Before the Day

    Two weeks out, every attendee receives a question to sit with, a note on how the day is built, and a badge with a QR code linked to a personal summit page.

    This is not a standard conference. Some moments ask something of you. All of them are optional. None of them are difficult.

    CoAct is a collaborative impact consultancy. It advises corporate clients on climate strategy, and refuses greenwashing and performative CSR. Ground Truth is built to hold its clients, and itself, to that same standard.

    9:40 a.m.
    Marcus Feld
    Group Chief Sustainability Officer, Halden Materials. Opening keynote.
    He walked through the same question wall as everyone else.
    A week earlier, his team was handed three lines from the wall, written by people who live beside his company's operations. His keynote opens with those three quotes. Thirty seconds of silence. Then he answers each one as if the person were in the room. The strategy builds from their words, not the brand's. Afterwards, attendees write one line on a card: "the target I'd set if I lived downwind." Feld reads five of them aloud. The room hears its own conscience alongside the plan.
    10:10 a.m., Dr Priya Nair, Carbon Integrity Lab

    The Makers Tables

    Every strategy is a balancing act. At the makers tables, teams stack the pieces of a plan: ambition, cost, evidence, dependency, and risk. Then they feel where it holds, and where it starts to tip.

    Drag the stones. Build your own balance, or take it apart.

    10:40 a.m., Refreshments
    What does our climate strategy look like from where you sit?
    Over refreshments, two kinds of table. At some, people from outside the field describe what they actually see in your plan: the gap between the claim and the reality, reflected back. At others, people who fundamentally disagree are seated together on a timer, to work the tension rather than smooth it away.
