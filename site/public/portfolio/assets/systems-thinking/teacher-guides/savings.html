teacher's guide · personal / financial

personal savings

build a financial buffer from less than one month to a sustainable reserve that absorbs surprises without derailing.

learning outcome

students distinguish container optimisations (interest rates, subscription cuts) from structural flow changes (auto-transfer, paycheque split) and from paradigm shifts (savings as freedom).

key concept

the dominant intervention is the paradigm-level reframing — savings as the purchase of future options, not deprivation. it wins because every spending decision is now filtered through a different question. structural changes (auto-transfer, paycheque split) help because they make saving the default. parameter changes (high-yield account, cancelled subscriptions) optimise the container or trim outflow at the margins; without changing the inflow rule or the underlying frame, they don't move the buffer.

common misconceptions to surface

  • "a better account fixes savings." returns matter only when the principal is non-trivial.
  • "cutting costs is the lever." freed cash gets absorbed by other spending unless structurally redirected.
  • "saving is sacrificing." in a redefined frame, saving is purchasing — and the question changes from "can I afford this?" to "is this worth more than a month of options?"

three debrief questions

  1. where in your finances does an opt-in behaviour need to become a default flow?
  2. what would change if you described your savings as a "freedom fund" out loud, to your household?
  3. which financial decisions are container-level optimisations dressed up as strategy?

lesson plans

15 minutes — solo play + 5 min debrief

  1. 10 min: solo play, commit with hypothesis.
  2. 5 min: name the tiers; share surprises.

30 minutes — solo play + pair discuss + debrief

  1. 10 min solo play.
  2. 10 min pair discussion: defaults vs. decisions in each pair's finances.
  3. 10 min group debrief.

60 minutes — context-set + solo + pair + group + transfer

  1. 10 min context-set: balancing loops in personal finance; the "near-zero attractor".
  2. 15 min solo play.
  3. 10 min pair discussion.
  4. 15 min group debrief.
  5. 10 min transfer prompt: rewrite one financial habit so that the default does the work.
