teacher's guide · personal

fitness

move from sporadic exercise to a sustainable weekly routine that doesn't collapse under life pressure.

learning outcome

students distinguish equipment and access changes from structural redesign, and evaluate identity-level shifts as the most durable lever in a personal balancing system.

key concept

the dominant intervention is the paradigm-level identity shift — from "I should exercise" to "this is who I am." it wins because every decision in the week (food, sleep, time, energy) is now filtered through a different question. parameter changes (shoes, gym membership) remove friction without creating habit; rules-level changes (a trainer, a redesigned week) work while the structure holds. identity-level change is the most durable because it makes new structure feel native rather than imposed.

common misconceptions to surface

  • "better gear builds the habit." gear removes friction at the margin; it does not create the schedule.
  • "more access produces more action." gym memberships are the canonical example of access without action.
  • "willpower is the bottleneck." structure beats willpower; identity beats both.

three debrief questions

  1. which of your behaviours is supported by an identity, and which is propped up by willpower?
  2. where in your week does exercise compete with everything else, rather than sit alongside it?
  3. what would change if you said "I am a runner" instead of "I'm trying to run more"?

lesson plans

15 minutes — solo play + 5 min debrief

  1. 10 min: solo play, commit with hypothesis.
  2. 5 min: name the tiers; share surprises.

30 minutes — solo play + pair discuss + debrief

  1. 10 min solo play.
  2. 10 min pair discussion: behaviour vs. identity in each pair's life.
  3. 10 min group debrief.

60 minutes — context-set + solo + pair + group + transfer

  1. 10 min context-set: balancing loops in personal systems; "structure beats willpower".
  2. 15 min solo play.
  3. 10 min pair discussion.
  4. 15 min group debrief.
  5. 10 min transfer prompt: name one identity statement you could honestly try on for the next month.
