      PEDAL Centre, University of Cambridge · 2025
      two weeks before

      before the day

      every attendee receives a question to sit with, a note about the day's design, and a name badge with a QR code linking to a personal conference page.

      this is not a standard conference. there are moments that ask something of you. all of them are optional. none of them are difficult.

      PEDAL (Play, Education, Development And Learning) is a research centre at the University of Cambridge. what follows shows how the format of their annual conference could be redesigned to embody the same principles the research advocates.

      2:15 p.m. — three tracks running simultaneously
      MAIN HALL

      AI panel

      a panel discussion on AI, toys, and the future of play. ran in the main hall as planned.

      instead of talking about AI and play, the audience would experience it. each table receives a toy and a prompt generated live by a language model. the panel watches the room play first, then responds to what they saw. the argument about AI and childhood stops being abstract.

      THE BOULIND

      the quiet room

      low light. no task. no facilitation. a place to sit with what the day had produced so far.

      some people need noise and conversation to process. some need stillness. both are legitimate. both were available. needing quiet is not opting out. it is a different way of being in.

      NETWORKING SPACE

      connecting play networks

      each table opened with one question, drawn from children's responses to being asked what they wish adults understood about play.

      a short paired exercise. map your own context.

      the template is there for people who think better with a structure in front of them. the conversation option is there for people who think better out loud.

      you leave with a map of your own context — named assets, named people, named gaps.
      ten minutes. no debrief required.
