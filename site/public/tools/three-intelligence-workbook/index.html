a self-assessment and practice workbook

three intelligences quietly shape how well you lead, decide, and work with others in business. most courses train one of them and assume the other two. this workbook is a way to see which you already have strong, which you're still building, and what to practise day-to-day to grow the ones that are thinner.

the semester arc

how to use this workbook across fifteen weeks.

week 1

rate yourself. plot your web. identify your two weakest subskills.

weeks 2–5

pick two personal practices and two school practices from your two weakest subskills. the practices that feel slightly uncomfortable are the ones working. track them in a note on your phone — which ones you tried, which ones stuck.

week 6

mid-semester re-rate. plot a new web in a different colour. compare.

weeks 7–14

rotate practices. the ones that became habit are done — pick new ones. the ones that didn't stick, drop them. replace with fresh ones.

week 15

final re-rate. plot your third web. note which subskills shifted and which didn't. the ones that didn't may need a different kind of practice than this workbook offers — talk to your professor, a counsellor, a peer.

rule of thumb. one subskill moves noticeably in a semester. two if you're disciplined. three means you weren't honest on the first rating.

why your professor gave you this

if your professor shared this workbook with you, they chose it because they believe these three intelligences belong in a business education — not as soft skills, but as the infrastructure underneath every case you analyse, every team you join, every decision you'll make as a future leader. some of the practices in here will feel obvious. some will feel foreign. the ones that feel foreign are the ones you need most.
