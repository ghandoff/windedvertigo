# Harbour Educational Gaps Analysis

> Date: 2026-04-03
> Context: Review of the five harbour games/apps against the depth.chart skills framework

---

|-----|------------------|---------------------|

---

## Identified Gaps (Against depth.chart 21 Skills)

### 1. Systems Thinking & Interconnection
No tool lets learners *experience* systems dynamics — feedback loops, emergence, unintended consequences. Core to the w.v philosophy ("interconnectedness of everything") but not yet playable.

### 2. Ethical Reasoning & Perspective-Taking
The "play, fair" initiative exists but has no harbour presence. No tool for navigating moral complexity, fairness dilemmas, or structured perspective-taking.

### 3. Scientific Inquiry & Evidence-Based Thinking
Vertigo-vault captures evidence, but no game teaches the *process* — observation, hypothesis, experimentation, revision. Gap given w.v's "pinpoint & prove" and "test & validate" quadrants.

### 4. Metacognition & Self-Regulation
Depth.chart is a reference, not a practice tool. No game helps learners observe their own thinking, set goals, or reflect on how they learn.

### 5. Narrative & Storytelling
No app focuses on constructing, sharing, or iterating on stories — despite w.v's work being fundamentally about "crafting compelling stories" and "visualizing insights from evidence."

### 6. Embodied / Physical Play Bridge
All five apps are digital. No tool bridges digital and physical play, despite "Find/Fold/Unfold/Find Again" being deeply physical.

---

## Recommendations — New Apps to Build

### Priority 1: tidal.pool — Systems Thinking Sandbox
Playful simulation where learners build and observe interconnected systems. Drop elements into a "pool" and watch emergent behaviors. Tinker with one variable, see ripple effects.
- **Targets:** systems thinking, cause-and-effect reasoning, complexity navigation
- **Connects to:** w.v philosophy of interconnectedness

### Priority 2: fold.fair — Ethical Reasoning Game
Brings "play, fair" to the harbour. Dilemma scenarios with no clean answers. Players weigh trade-offs, hear perspectives, make choices, see what others chose. Not a morality quiz — a space for genuine tension.
- **Targets:** ethical reasoning, perspective-taking, empathy, negotiation
- **Connects to:** "play, fair" studio initiative

### Priority 3: storycraft — Collaborative Narrative Builder
Construct stories from evidence, data, and experience. Drag fragments (quotes, data points, images) onto a canvas and weave into a narrative arc. Could integrate with vertigo-vault.
- **Targets:** narrative construction, communication, evidence synthesis, collaboration
- **Connects to:** "pinpoint & prove" quadrant, vertigo-vault evidence

### Priority 4: mirror.log — Metacognitive Reflection Tool
Lightweight journaling companion that prompts learners to notice *how* they're thinking. Pairs with any harbour game — after a session, asks: "what surprised you? what did you notice about your own thinking?" Builds personal learning patterns over time.
- **Targets:** metacognition, self-regulation, growth mindset, reflective practice
- **Connects to:** depth.chart skills (makes them practicable, not just reference)

### Priority 5: paper.trail — Physical-Digital Bridge
Activity toolkit starting in the physical world (fold, cut, build, observe) using camera/device to capture, annotate, share. The "Find/Fold/Unfold/Find Again" process made interactive. Gateway experience for the entire harbour.
- **Targets:** embodied cognition, observation, documentation
- **Connects to:** brand philosophy, creaseworks physical mailer
