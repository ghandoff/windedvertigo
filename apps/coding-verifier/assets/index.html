layer 1 · extraction — is the evidence reproducibly there?

every claim's evidence is extracted twice, independently and blind, against the same codebook (the programme-theory conjectures); a named human then confirms it against the source. agreement shows the evidence is reproducibly present in the sources — not one reader's impression. disagreements are routed to a human to adjudicate.

coder a
first independent extraction — pulls verbatim quotes + source locations against the codebook.
coder b
a second, independent, blind re-extraction of the same sources, without seeing coder a. blindness is what makes agreement meaningful.
cARL
a third independent read on the highest-stakes claims, plus winded.vertigo's expert eye on the evidence base.

layer 2 · interpretation — how do we read the verified evidence?

only claims whose evidence passed layer 1 (verified or adjudicated) appear here. the same verified evidence is read through three complementary lenses, so the reading is both reproducible (layer 1) and richly contextual (layer 2). divergence between lenses is a feature — it surfaces perspective-dependent nuance rather than error.

psychometric
measurement rigour — how strong is the empirical basis (construct, sample, quantification, generalisability)?
practitioner
practice plausibility — does this hold up in delivery and the field; is it feasible and contextually true?
collective
winded.vertigo's expertise — how does it fit the broader evidence base, threshold concepts, and theory of change?
