soak sketches

soak = liquid being absorbed by another material. 10 different substrates and moments. each ~7–11 elements, matching the simplicity of drip's base/pool/pour.

paper spot

~7 elements

clean circular stain on a sheet of paper. drip in its simplest absorbed form.

bleed

~11 elements

watercolor bloom — irregular organic stain with feathered edges and tiny spatter dots. painterly.

sponge

~12 elements

drip soaked into a porous sponge. holes visible through the wet patch. most textural of the set.

wicking

~10 elements

capillary action. a cloth strip stands in a small pool, water climbs UP the strip against gravity. dynamic and unexpected.

inkblot

~10 elements

symmetric rorschach-style blot, bilateral across a faint fold-crease line. analytical and a little uncanny.

coffee ring

~8 elements

the physics effect where a drying drop concentrates its edge darker than its center. three concentric tones, no feathering.

dripping in

~8 elements

caught mid-action: a drop falling onto paper while a small wet spot has already started forming at impact.

napkin corner

~11 elements

stain entering a patterned napkin from the bottom-right corner, spreading inward. emergency-dinner-spill energy.

tissue

~9 elements

delicate tissue paper with a very diffused stain — multiple soft layers fading outward. dreamy / quiet.

dirt

~11 elements

puddle on the ground, with a visibly darker brown wet-ring showing water absorbed into the surrounding earth.
