mud sketch — v1

  • Archetype: the patient shaper. Validates "it's okay to be slow and to change your mind." Mud is the character who sits with an idea until it becomes something.
  • Form: malleable / granular. Hosts DB form_primary values like malleable, volume, substrate, clay, dough, putty, plasticine, mud, silt, sand, granular, foam.
  • Motion vocabulary: slow amorphous shifts, no sharp edges. .slowshift runs an 11-second cycle of subtle scale (±1%) and rotation (±0.8°) — the body gently breathes/settles. .sleepyblink fires every 7.5s with a quick scaleY squish. The animation-delay variants (.a, .b, .c) stagger the cards so they don't all drift in sync — reinforces "each one is its own slow moment."
  • Mud's motion principle is even more extreme than Crate's: stillness is the rule, and even the motion is stillness-adjacent. No thunks, no twitches, no drifts across the canvas — just a faint, continuous shape-memory adjustment.
  • Silhouette rule: all curves, no corners. Every outline uses Q curves. The one exception — the star mould impression in the mould pose — is deliberate: the sharp geometry reads as an alien shape that was pressed INTO the otherwise soft body, which is exactly the function being demonstrated.
  • Tell: a thumbprint impression — a small rotated oval depression (outer --mud-dk + inner --mud-deep slot + a tiny highlight rim on the lower edge). Always positioned slightly left of centre on the upper body surface. Rotated -12° for a "pressed with a thumb tip" angle. Visible on all four poses. Scale stays consistent at rx=6.5, ry=4.2 so it reads as the same fingerprint across the cast.
  • Secondary silhouette detail: a ground-contact pool ellipse beneath the body on every pose — reinforces mud's weight and its relationship with the surface it's on (mud sits, sags, settles).
  • Palette: --mud #a6794a (warm mid-brown body), --mud-lt #c89a6d (top highlight crest), --mud-dk #6e4a20 (thumbprint outer + under-belly shadow), --mud-deep #4f3414 (thumbprint inner slot + ground-contact pool). Feels earthy, warm, not muddy-grey. Reads as clay/earth, not dirt.
  • Eyes: half-closed sleepy lids (Q-curve line over a small filled dot) in place of the round open eyes used by other characters. Conveys "contemplative, not asleep — just patient." Eyes are placed on the right side of the body to avoid fighting the tell for attention.
  • Function poses: base (a proper illustrated puddle — flat wet pool with pebbles, reflections, grass tufts on the edges — reads as "mud at rest, settled"), shape (pinched-and-pulled rabbit — a single continuous outline traces chest → nose → ears → back → belly, so it reads as sculpted-from-one-lump rather than built from clumps), mould (sandcastle pressed from the same mud — two low battlements + a taller middle tower with a flag — demonstrates "pressed against a form"), stick (marshmallow-and-straw bridge — sticks engineered with mud blobs joining them — demonstrates adhesion). The dramatic variance across the four poses is intentional: it illustrates the slow shape that doesn't mind changing its mind. Morpher ships with a 1200ms cubic-eased path-tween (slower than Crate's 900ms — mud takes its time). The silhouette tweens as a single closed path between all four poses; pose-specific extras (pebbles, inner-ear shading, battlements, truss sticks) clear during the morph and re-render when it settles, so the animation stays legible. All four silhouettes share the same topology (simple closed loop) so the polyline tween doesn't self-cross — the stick pose uses a gentle arched puddle with sticks as fg decoration rather than a hole-in-middle bridge shape.
  • Adult mode: same silhouette, sand palette, tell still present but muted. Eyes fully open as dots (not sleepy) so the grown-up version reads as focused rather than dreaming.
