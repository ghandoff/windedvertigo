what changed between v4 and v5 — the two big ones

  • twist lines now follow the rope. replaced the stroke="url(#pattern)" approach (which painted the rope with screen-fixed diagonals — hence the "background" feel) with path-aware ticks. a JS function walks each rope's centerline using getPointAtLength(), computes the tangent at each step, builds a perpendicular vector, rotates it by 28°, and draws one short line from inner edge to outer edge. one diagonal line per twist, anchored to the rope's sides — exactly what you described.
  • real path tween. the morpher now does actual interpolation à la Flash. on page load, each pose's path is sampled into 48 equally-spaced points along its arc length. clicking the morpher runs a 900ms requestAnimationFrame loop that lerps point-by-point between the current sample array and the next, rebuilding an interpolated path every frame. no external library — it's ~30 lines of math. result: cord actually bends/stretches/reorganises between shapes, rather than fading through intermediates.

how the "lower res during transition" works

  • the user intuited that this should cost something — it doesn't, because no images exist. it's 48 float pairs × 3 paths, updated per frame. that's negligible.
  • but I still lower the fidelity visually because it feels right: during the morph the SVG gets filter: blur(2.5px), the wobble filter on the outline is temporarily removed (saves feDisplacementMap cost per frame), eyes + ticks disappear (they'd jitter), and the outline opacity drops slightly. all restored on settle.
  • that blur isn't just optimization — it's the "dream dissolve" that makes the transition feel like metamorphosis rather than a mechanical shape-swap.

the twist-tick math, for anyone curious

  • getPointAtLength(d) gives you a point on the path at arc-distance d.
  • differencing two nearby points (at d-ε and d+ε) gives the tangent direction — i.e., which way the rope is running at that spot.
  • rotating the tangent 90° gives perpendicular-to-rope (straight across). rotating that by 28° gives the rope-twist direction. drawing a line from -halfWidth to +halfWidth along that direction crosses the rope at the right angle.
  • because the computation uses the path's local tangent, ticks automatically follow curves. on the nest ring, ticks radiate around. on the bridge sag, they follow the sag. on the skip-rope arc, they arc.
