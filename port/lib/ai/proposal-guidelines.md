# winded.vertigo — Proposal Draft Guidelines

These guidelines govern all proposals auto-generated by the RFP Radar pipeline when an opportunity moves to "pursuing" status. They apply to the AI system prompt in `proposal-generator.ts` and to any human editing of draft proposals.

---

## Brand Voice & Style

- **Brand name:** always `winded.vertigo` (lowercase, period between words). Abbreviate as `w.v`. Never "Winded Vertigo", "WindedVertigo", or "winded vertigo".
- **Sentence case:** use proper sentence capitalisation throughout. The lowercase aesthetic applies to the brand name, not to all copy.
- **Tone:** direct, principled, evidence-grounded. Name problems honestly. Do not use corporate filler ("we are pleased to submit", "robust and holistic", "leverage synergies", "impactful outcomes"). Write as a firm with a point of view.
- **First person:** use "we" and "our" naturally. Do not open every sentence with "winded.vertigo".
- **Audience:** proposals are reviewed by programme officers, procurement leads, and foundation staff — often under time pressure. Clarity and confidence matter more than length.

---

## Proposal Structure

Every proposal must contain these sections in this order:

| # | Section | Format |
|---|---------|--------|
| 0 | **Callout block** | RFP name, client, due date, type |
| 1 | **Executive Summary** | 1–2 prose paragraphs |
| 2 | **Understanding of Requirements** | 1–2 prose paragraphs |
| 3 | **Proposed Approach** | Prose paragraphs; phases may be numbered if the work is clearly phased |
| 4 | **Relevant Experience** | Prose paragraphs weaving in specific BD assets |
| 5 | **Team** | Bolded names, prose sentences per person |
| 6 | **Value Proposition** | 1–2 prose paragraphs |
| 7 | **Clarifying Questions for Client** | Bulleted list of 3–5 discrete questions |
| 8 | **Gaps to Fill Before Submitting** | One sentence per gap, each tagged with a responsible team member and due date |
| 9 | **References** | Full APA citations for every source cited inline |

---

## Prose vs. Selective Lists

Default is full prose throughout. Selective bullets are permitted — and preferred — when:

- Listing **three or more discrete parallel items** a reviewer needs to scan quickly (e.g., data collection instruments, evaluation levels, workshop modules)
- The items are genuinely parallel and would be harder to parse as prose than as a list

**Never use bullets in:** Executive Summary, Understanding of Requirements, Relevant Experience, Team, or Value Proposition. These sections make arguments — arguments live in prose, not lists.

---

## Visual Aide Suggestions

Include a `🎨 Visual aide` callout block wherever a diagram, table, or infographic would materially help a reviewer understand a complex relationship. Use this format:

```
🎨 Visual aide: [description of what the diagram shows and why it earns its place here]
```

Common cases where visual aides are warranted:
- A **theory of change or logic model** diagram at the end of the Understanding section
- A **phased approach timeline** or swimlane at the end of the Proposed Approach section
- A **transfer rate comparison** (citing Joyce & Showers) for professional development proposals
- A **competency framework matrix** when introducing a bespoke competency model
- An **MEL feedback loop diagram** for monitoring and evaluation engagements

One well-placed visual aide is better than several generic ones.

---

## Citations

- Use **only real citations** from peer-reviewed journals, edited academic volumes, or authoritative policy reports (UNICEF, OECD, World Bank, government ministries, Learning Policy Institute).
- No invented or hallucinated citations. If unsure whether a citation is accurate, omit it.
- Format: `(Author, year)` inline; full APA reference in the References section.
- Italicise titles in the References section.
- Every cited source must also be added as a new entry in the w.v Annotated Bibliography: `https://www.notion.so/c6c6da5373874fedb469f5f350da5aed`

## Gaps to Fill Before Submitting

Each gap must:

1. Be one clear prose sentence identifying what is missing and why it blocks the proposal.
2. End with a tagged assignee and target date: `→ **[Name]**: [action] by [date].`
3. Be created as a task in the w.v tasks database (`collection://224e4ee7-4ba4-81c9-9fa7-000b194bb2fd`) with:
   - `task`: imperative description of the action
   - `task type`: `research`, `coordinate`, or `plan` as appropriate
   - `status`: `in queue`
   - `priority`: `high` if the gap would block submission; `medium` otherwise
   - `due date`: at least 3 days before the RFP due date
   - `owner`: responsible team member's Notion user ID

## Quality Checklist

Before finalising any proposal draft:

- [ ] Brand name is `winded.vertigo` throughout (not "Winded Vertigo" or "winded vertigo")
- [ ] No bullet lists in Executive Summary, Understanding, Experience, Team, or Value Proposition
- [ ] Selective bullets used where they genuinely help readability
- [ ] Visual aide suggestion included where a diagram earns its place
- [ ] All citations are real and verifiable
- [ ] All cited sources added to the annotated bibliography
- [ ] Team section names specific people, not generic role labels
- [ ] Gaps section includes tagged assignees with dates
- [ ] Tasks created in the tasks database for each gap
- [ ] References section present with full APA citations
