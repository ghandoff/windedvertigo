# revenue-marketing alignment — winded.vertigo

> how marketing activities translate to signed contracts.
> last updated: may 2026

## the pipeline

w.v sells consulting engagements and programme design, not SaaS. the sales cycle is 4–12 weeks from first touch to signed contract, typically shorter for warm referrals and longer for institutional/government buyers.

### pipeline stages

| stage | definition | marketing's role | typical duration |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **awareness** | prospect knows w.v exists | content, social, conference presence, PR | ongoing |
| **engagement** | prospect interacts with w.v content or product | harbour signups, substack subscribers, webinar attendees, whirlpool guests | 1–4 weeks |
| **conversation** | direct dialogue initiated | warm intro, cold outreach reply, inbound enquiry, event follow-up | 1–2 weeks |
| **proposal** | w.v submits a formal proposal or SOW | proposal generator (port CRM), case studies, reference clients | 1–4 weeks |
| **contract** | signed agreement + PO | — (finance/ops) | 1–2 weeks |

### what counts as a marketing-qualified lead (MQL)

for w.v, an MQL is a person or organisation that:
1. has engaged with w.v content or product at least twice (e.g., harbour signup + substack open, or conference conversation + website visit)
2. fits one of our target audience segments (institutional buyer, higher ed faculty, learning designer, or k-12 decision-maker)
3. has a plausible budget path (is at an organisation that buys consulting, not an individual teacher with no procurement authority)

MQLs are tracked in port CRM. the threshold is low because w.v's total addressable market is narrow — every qualified lead matters.

## revenue attribution model

w.v is too small for complex multi-touch attribution. use a simple **first-touch + last-touch** model:

- **first touch:** how did the prospect first hear about w.v? (conference, referral, cold email, social, search)
- **last touch:** what triggered the proposal? (direct conversation, event, inbound enquiry)

track both in port CRM on each contact record. quarterly review of attribution data informs where to invest marketing effort.

| average contract value | $40-60k | sizing signal |

### the math
$500k target ÷ $50k average contract = 10 contracts needed in 5 months = 2/month.
at 40% win rate, that's 5 proposals/month.
at 60% conversation-to-proposal rate, that's ~8 meaningful conversations/month.
at 15% cold outreach reply rate + 40% warm reply rate, that's ~30 outreach touches/week blended.

this is achievable with the current team if payton owns execution and garrett owns relationships.

## weekly CMO check (built into wednesday whirlpool)

every wednesday, review:
1. how many outreach touches this week? (target: 30)
2. how many replies / conversations started? (target: 4-6)
3. how many proposals in flight? (target: 2-3 at any time)
4. any content published? (target: at least 1)
5. pipeline value movement — anything closer to signing?

this takes 10 minutes. if it's not happening, the $500k target is drifting.
