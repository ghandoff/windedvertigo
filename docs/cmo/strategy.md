## executive summary

winded.vertigo is at an inflection point. we have products, clients, and a platform. what we lack is visibility and momentum outside our warm network. the gap between where we are (zero external marketing presence, $34k cash, 4-month runway) and where we need to be ($500k in signed contracts by september) is real. but it's not impossible.

this strategy bridges that gap by treating marketing as a direct revenue function. every campaign, every post, every email is tied to a specific contract opportunity or pipeline stage. we're not building brand in a vacuum—we're building *credibility* in the communities and conferences where our clients and prospects live.

the plan has three layers:
1. **immediate activation** (may): warm network outreach + ppcs→harbour funnel + content launch
2. **amplification** (june–july): conference presence + harbour community building + cold outreach refresh
3. **scaling** (august–september): leverage early wins into retainer relationships + product revenue seeds

success metric: $500k in new contracts signed by september 1. secondary metrics: 2,000 email subscribers, 1,000 harbour signups, 50+ media mentions/conference placements.

financial reality check: we have ~4 months to prove the model works. this is an existence-level priority. every person on this team knows that. the marketing strategy below is designed to make that real without sugarcoating the odds.

## 1. market positioning

### where we sit

winded.vertigo occupies a rare position in the global learning ecosystem:
- we're a **learning design collective**, not a consulting firm (which means we are practitioner-grounded, culturally embedded, and resistant to generic solutions)
- we build **playful learning ecosystems** backed by research (our PPCS evidence infrastructure)
- we operate across the **public good space** (PRME, UNICEF, IDB) where our playfulness philosophy has institutional credibility
- we have a **technology moat** (harbour: 19 games, depth-chart, magic-link auth, R2 image infrastructure)
- we maintain **cultural appropriateness** as a non-negotiable design principle (maria's influence embedded in every product)

### competitive differentiation

| dimension | winded.vertigo | competitors |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| **design philosophy** | playful, joyful, culturally embedded | productivity-first, generalist platforms |
| **evidence base** | PPCS (pedagogy certificate system, UN-backed) | case studies, anecdotal |
| **team model** | geographically distributed collective with deep practice | centralized, consultant-driven |
| **product ecosystem** | integrated (harbour → creaseworks → ppcs → evidence) | point solutions |
| **price point** | premium (research-backed justifies it) | race-to-bottom SaaS, underbid consulting |
| **go-to-market** | community-first, relationship-driven | advertising, lead gen, cold sales |

### narrative positioning

we tell three overlapping stories, depending on audience:

**for educators + practitioners:** harbour is the games you've always wanted—beautiful, joyful, proven to work, and designed *with* your community, not *at* it.

**for institutions (UN, ministries, NGOs):** winded.vertigo is the learning design partner that brings research rigour, cultural sensitivity, and community trust. we reduce deployment risk and accelerate adoption through our collective model.

**for funders + investors:** we're a sustainable, profitable alternative to the ed-tech arms race. our model trades VC scale for long-term client relationships and product reuse. our margins are better. our churn is lower.

## 5. content strategy

### content pillars

**pillar 1: playful learning evidence**
- why play matters (research backed)
- pedagogy of joy
- cultural appropriateness as design principle
- examples from harbour + client work

**pillar 2: collective practice**
- how we design (collaborative + transparent)
- practitioner stories (who we work with, what we learn)
- challenges + lessons
- behind-the-scenes of winded.vertigo

**pillar 3: technology for good**
- harbour as case study (19 games, open design, low-bandwidth)
- learning evidence at scale (PPCS infrastructure)
- ed-tech that actually centres communities (not extractive)

**pillar 4: practitioner resources**
- free guides, toolkits, templates
- harbour activity guides
- playful learning facilitation tips
- downloadable evidence frameworks

### content formats

| format | channel | frequency | owner | purpose |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------|---------|
| **substack essays** | substack + linkedin | 2x/month | jamie (play), lamis/maria (practice) | deep thinking, email list growth |
| **social posts** | instagram + bluesky + linkedin | 3–4x/week | payton (visual), garrett (reflection) | real-time engagement, community |
| **video clips** | instagram reels, linkedin | 2x/month | payton | high engagement, demo harbour |
| **case studies** | website + email | 1x/quarter | garrett + client | social proof, sales enablement |
| **guest posts** | external publications | 1x/quarter | garrett + team | authority, reach outside network |
| **webinars** | zoom + email | 1x/quarter | lamis + garrett + press play | lead generation, thought leadership |
| **practitioner guides** | website + gumroad | 1x/quarter | maria + team | free resources, lead magnets |

### how whirlpool becomes content

**mechanism:** every whirlpool session (mon + wed, 4pm utc) is documented + mined for content.
- **during:** lamis/payton capture key quotes, themes, practitioner insights
- **after:** garrett synthesizes into 1–2 social posts + 1 newsletter snippet
- **quarterly:** compile best practitioner insights into "lessons from whirlpool" blog post or substack essay

**effect:** weekly community event becomes ongoing content engine. no extra work; just intentional documentation.

### how harbour apps are content vehicles

**mechanism:** each harbour game is a content asset.
- **per app:** 2–3 social posts (game mechanic, learning principle, classroom use case)
- **quarterly:** app spotlight (deep dive on design process, pedagogy, evidence of impact)
- **ongoing:** user-generated content (educator screenshots, student wins, testimonials)

**effect:** 19 games = 38–57 pieces of potential social content over 12 months, without any new design work.
