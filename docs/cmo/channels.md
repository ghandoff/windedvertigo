# Marketing Channels & Platform Strategy

## Overview
winded.vertigo operates across six primary channels, each with distinct purpose, cadence, audience, and ownership. This document defines the strategy, tone, KPIs, and execution model for each.

---

## LinkedIn

**Purpose**
Establish Garrett as a thought leader in learning design, pedagogy, and institutional impact. Position w.v as a trusted partner for universities, corporate L&D, foundations, and government agencies. Drive awareness and credibility for PPCS, institutional partnerships, and speaking engagements.

**Audience**
- Higher ed faculty and administrators
- Corporate L&D directors and learning designers
- EdTech founders and product managers
- Institutional buyers (UN, foundations, government)
- Conference organisers and professional networks

**Cadence**
- 2–3 posts per week (mix of long-form articles, insights, research links, company updates)
- Articles: 1–2 per month (1200–1800 words, on pedagogy, evidence, institutional trends)
- Engagement: daily replies to comments, Q&A in relevant threads

**Tone**
- Authoritative but approachable
- Evidence-based; cite research, data, client outcomes
- Honest about challenges and trade-offs in learning design
- Personal but professional; Garrett's perspective comes through
- Avoid hype; focus on substance

**Content Types**
- Research insights: "3 things we learned from 500 hours of classroom observation"
- Case studies: "How we helped [institution] redesign [programme] using..."
- Trend analysis: emerging topics in learning tech, pedagogy, accreditation
- Resource shares: w.v research outputs, whirlpool recordings, tool recommendations
- Team spotlights: "Meet Lamis, who designed the PPCS assessment framework"
- Institutional wins: PRME contracts, IDB Salvador partnership, Sesame Workshop engagement
- Thought leadership on pedagogy, fairness, play-based learning

**Key Metrics**
- Monthly: article impressions (target 2k–5k), engagement rate (6–10%), follower growth (20–30 net new/month)
- Quarterly: inbound leads from LinkedIn (track via port CRM source), speaking invitations, partnership enquiries
- Benchmark: LinkedIn Sales Navigator engagement, response rates from target accounts (institutional buyers, higher ed)

**Owner**
- Strategy & voice: Garrett (with Lamis for content ideas)
- Execution: Payton (posts, scheduling, engagement)
- Content: Jamie (writing support), Lamis (research inputs)

**Tools**
- LinkedIn native scheduler or Buffer
- Port CRM (lead tracking from LinkedIn)
- Google Docs (draft articles before posting)

## Instagram

**Purpose**
Build community with parents, teachers, and creative educators. Showcase w.v's philosophy on play, creativity, and learning. Drive engagement through relatable, visual, short-form content. Capture email addresses and convert to email list. Position w.v as a trusted voice in parent education and classroom creativity.

**Audience**
- Parents aged 25–55 (primarily mothers, early childhood educators)
- K–12 teachers seeking classroom inspiration
- Homeschooling families
- Creative practitioners interested in play-based learning

**Cadence**
- 4–5 posts per week (mix of photos, carousel posts, Reels)
- Reels: 2–3 per week (15–60 seconds, trending audio, practical tips)
- Stories: daily during campaign windows (whirlpool announcements, Q&A, polls)
- Engagement: 2–3 hour daily window for replies, DM responses

**Tone**
- Warm, relatable, non-judgmental
- Permission-giving: "It's okay if your kids are bored. It's okay if you're not a crafty parent."
- Visual, concrete, immediately actionable
- Celebrate messiness and imperfection
- Authentic behind-the-scenes (Garrett's studio, Gina with kids, team moments)

**Content Types**
- How-to: "5-minute rainy day activity" (carousel, step-by-step photos)
- Reels: quick tips on play, creativity, screen time, boredom, development milestones
- Testimonials: parent and teacher stories (video or quote graphic)
- Research-backed tips: "Science says boredom is good. Here's why." (link to w.v blog/research)
- Quotes & memes: relatable parenting/teaching humour
- Events: whirlpool announcements, free workshop signups, new resource drops
- Behind-the-scenes: studio clips, team moments, Garrett/Lamis/Maria at events
- User-generated content: retag parents sharing w.v activities with their kids

**Key Metrics**
- Weekly: reach (target 1k–3k per post), engagement rate (8–12%), saves (indicator of share-worthiness)
- Monthly: new followers (target 50–100), website clicks, email signups via bio link
- Quarterly: conversion from Instagram to email list, whirlpool attendance from IG, repeat engagement rate

**Owner**
- Strategy & tone: Payton (with Garrett for philosophy alignment)
- Execution: Payton (posting, Reels, Stories, engagement)
- Content: Payton (photography, captions), Jamie (writing support), Gina (behind-the-scenes footage)

**Tools**
- Later or Buffer (scheduling)
- Canva (graphics, Reels templates)
- Linktree or simple link-in-bio (email capture via Substack or Resend signup form)

## Bluesky (Edu Community)

**Purpose**
Participate in the emerging educator community on Bluesky. Build early-adopter credibility. Share research, pedagogy ideas, and w.v updates in a less corporate, more conversational space. Drive awareness among progressive educators and learning technologists.

**Audience**
- Progressive K–12 educators
- EdTech innovators and early adopters
- Learning researchers and academics
- Online education community leaders

**Cadence**
- 3–4 posts per week (shorter, more conversational than LinkedIn)
- Mix of thoughts, research links, thread replies, event announcements
- Engagement: responsive to replies and mentions

**Tone**
- Conversational, idea-sharing
- Curious and open to debate
- Authentic perspective on pedagogy and learning design
- Less polished than LinkedIn; more thinking-out-loud

**Content Types**
- Thoughts on learning trends, pedagogy, assessment
- Research findings and links
- Whirlpool announcements and event highlights
- Replies to education threads (building community)
- Reblasts of educator voices, research, tools
- Playful/behind-the-scenes moments

**Key Metrics**
- Weekly: reply count, thread engagement
- Monthly: follower growth (target 20–40 net new), engagement rate (10–15%)
- Quarterly: link clicks to w.v resources, whirlpool signups from Bluesky

**Owner**
- Garrett (primary voice, replying to community threads)
- Payton (scheduling and content capture)

**Tools**
- Bluesky native app or Puffin (scheduling tool, if available)

## Substack

**Purpose**
Publish long-form thought leadership and research insights. Build a direct-to-reader email list. Establish w.v as a source of evidence-based learning design thinking. Drive awareness and credibility for PPCS, research labs, institutional partnerships.

**Audience**
- Learning designers and instructional designers
- Educators seeking deeper pedagogy resources
- EdTech founders and product managers
- Institutional buyers and programme officers

**Cadence**
- 2 essays per month (1500–2500 words each)
- Consistent day/time (e.g., Thursday 6pm PT)
- Mix of original research, case studies, methodology guides, field observations

**Tone**
- Authoritative, evidence-based
- Narrative and personal (Garrett's voice and perspective)
- Substantive; no fluff or hype
- Honest about limitations and trade-offs
- References and links to research

**Content Types**
- Deep dives: "The evidence base for play-based learning (and what it doesn't tell us)"
- Methodology: "How we designed the PPCS assessment framework" (step-by-step)
- Case studies: "Building institutional buy-in for pedagogical change: lessons from 3 universities"
- Research synthesis: "What the latest neuroscience tells us about creativity and learning"
- Field notes: observations from whirlpool, client work, research labs
- Commentary: response to industry trends, policy, or emerging challenges

**Key Metrics**
- Monthly: subscribers (target 200–400 net new), open rate (target 45–55%), click-through rate (15–20%)
- Quarterly: conversion to whirlpool attendance, port CRM leads, speaking invitations
- Benchmark: reader feedback, republish requests, external citations

**Owner**
- Strategy & writing: Garrett (with Lamis for research/ideas)
- Editorial: Payton (scheduling, promotion, list management)

**Tools**
- Substack native platform
- Google Docs (drafting)
- Resend (optional: email list sync)

## Email Campaigns (via Resend + Port CRM)

**Purpose**
Nurture relationships with educators, designers, institutional contacts. Share curated resources, whirlpool announcements, research updates, webinar invitations. Convert subscribers to whirlpool participants, workshop attendees, and institutional partnerships.

**Audience**
- K–12 educators (teachers, curriculum leads, school leaders)
- Higher ed faculty
- Parents and family learning advocates
- Learning designers and corporate L&D
- Institutional programme officers and foundation contacts

**Cadence**
- Weekly digest: Thursdays, 9am PT (curated w.v updates, whirlpool highlights, resource recommendations, research link)
- Announcement campaigns: whirlpool signups (launch 2 weeks prior), major initiatives (PRME, IDB, research drops), webinar invitations
- Segmented campaigns: educators vs. institutional buyers vs. parents (different tone, offers, CTAs)

**Tone**
- Warm, authentic, conversational
- Practical and actionable
- Personal perspective from w.v team (not corporate)
- Clear CTAs; respect subscriber time

**Content Types**
- Weekly digest: top 3–5 resources, whirlpool summary, team update, one research highlight
- Whirlpool invitations: agenda, learning outcomes, speaker bios, registration link
- New resource drops: "We've published a free guide to..." (lead magnet)
- Event announcements: conferences, workshops, research releases
- Seasonal campaigns: back-to-school (teachers), New Year (parents/professionals), summer (families)
- Feedback requests: post-whirlpool survey, product feedback, topic suggestions

**Key Metrics**
- Weekly: open rate (target 35–45%), click-through rate (8–12%), unsubscribe rate (< 0.3%)
- Monthly: subscriber growth (net new from landing page, lead magnets), list health (engagement segmentation)
- Quarterly: conversion to whirlpool attendance, institutional enquiries, product adoption

**Owner**
- Strategy & copy: Garrett (with Payton for tone check)
- Execution: Payton (segmentation, scheduling, A/B testing)
- Content: Jamie (writing), Lamis (research/curation)

**Tools**
- Resend (email platform, hosted on w.v domain)
- Port CRM (subscriber management, segmentation, lead tracking)
- Google Drive (draft templates, campaign planning)

## Conferences & Speaking

**Purpose**
Establish w.v expertise at key industry venues. Build relationships with practitioners, institutional buyers, and partner organisations. Generate leads and partnerships. Amplify w.v research and products through live demos and workshops.

**Audience**
- Conference attendees (teachers, faculty, learning designers, institutional leaders)
- Conference organisers and speaker networks
- Industry partners and potential collaborators
- Media and industry reporters

**Cadence**
- Target: 4–6 speaking engagements per year (mix of panel, breakout session, workshop, keynote)
- Quarterly: scout upcoming calls for proposals (ASCD, AERA, ISTE, SXSW Edu, eLearning Guild, regional associations)
- Monthly: respond to speaking invitations and RFP deadlines

**Key Events (2026 target)**
- ASCD Annual Conference (April–May)
- AERA (spring or fall)
- eLearning Guild events (distributed throughout year)
- SXSW Edu (March, 2027)
- NSTA and subject-specific conferences
- Regional teacher association events
- EdTech conferences (ISTE, online events)

**Session Types**
- Breakout sessions: "Designing for depth in the age of crowded curricula" (60–90 min)
- Workshops: hands-on w.v product demo (creaseworks, depth-chart)
- Panels: learning design trends, evidence-based pedagogy, institutional change
- Keynotes: future of learning design, role of play and creativity
- Poster sessions: research from Play & Fair labs, PPCS evidence

**Tone**
- Expert but accessible
- Evidence-based; cite research and client outcomes
- Interactive; involve audience (Q&A, activities, reflection)
- Practical takeaways; attendees leave with concrete actions

**Promotion**
- Speaker website page (link in email, LinkedIn, Substack)
- Social media amplification: LinkedIn posts, Instagram Stories, Bluesky thread
- Email campaign to list: "Garrett is speaking at ASCD — here's why you should attend"
- Post-event: key quotes to social, Substack reflection, attendee follow-up

**Key Metrics**
- Per session: attendance, feedback score (target 4.5+/5), Q&A engagement
- Quarterly: leads generated (via session sign-up sheet or post-event email), partnership enquiries
- Annual: speaking fee revenue (if applicable), brand lift (LinkedIn mentions, referral rate)

**Owner**
- Strategy & submissions: Garrett (with Payton for tracking)
- Execution: Payton (scheduling, logistics, follow-up), Jamie (slide design, abstracts)
- Follow-up: Payton (attendee outreach, CRM capture)

**Tools**
- Google Sheets (CFP tracker: deadline, status, session topic, speaking fee)
- Port CRM (attendee capture, post-event nurture)
- Notion (speaker deck templates, session planning)
