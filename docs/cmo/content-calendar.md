# Content Calendar Framework

## Purpose
The content calendar translates w.v's strategic channels and audience segments into a repeatable, cross-platform rhythm. It aligns w.v's institutional calendar (whirlpool cycles, speaking season, research releases, product launches) with audience rhythms (back-to-school for educators, academic calendar for faculty, parenting seasons for families). It defines what gets made once and flows across six channels, reducing production overhead and maximising reach.

---

## Annual Rhythm

### Quarterly Themes & Institutional Events

**Q1 (Jan–Mar): Deep Pedagogy & Institutional Change**
- Theme: "Evidence over hype—designing learning systems that last"
- Key events: AERA call for proposals (due ~mid-Jan), strategic planning season (higher ed budgets), new funding announcements
- Content focus: Research synthesis, case studies from recent client work (IDB Salvador, PRME implementations), accreditation readiness (AACSB, PRME)
- Product highlight: PPCS, depth-chart methodology
- Campaigns: Higher ed institutional buyers, faculty networks

**Q2 (Apr–Jun): Play, Creativity, & Classroom Practice**
- Theme: "Learning that sticks—what play-based design actually looks like in practice"
- Key events: ASCD Annual Conference (Apr–May), Harbour launch (28 May), summer prep season, teacher professional learning windows
- Content focus: Classroom case studies, teacher testimonials, how-to guides, summer learning strategies, whirlpool highlights
- Product highlight: creaseworks, depth-chart classroom adaptations, Play & Fair labs research
- Campaigns: K–12 educators, parents planning summer

**Q3 (Jul–Sep): Back-to-School & Professional Renewal**
- Theme: "Ready to redesign—back-to-school prep that actually changes practice"
- Key events: Summer learning initiatives wrap, back-to-school purchasing season, ISTE (early Aug sometimes), teacher prep season
- Content focus: Lesson plan templates, curriculum mapping, design-sprint guides, testimonials from teachers who redesigned courses over summer, Whirlpool back-to-school editions
- Product highlight: creaseworks templates, depth-chart, harbour suite rollout messaging
- Campaigns: K–12 educators (highest intensity), corporate L&D (post-summer refresh), parents (end of summer)

**Q4 (Oct–Dec): Evidence & Year-End Impact**
- Theme: "What we learned—2026 research highlights and reflections"
- Key events: Play & Fair labs findings release, annual impact report, holiday giving season, year-end institutional planning (foundations, government budgets for next fiscal), eLearning Guild events
- Content focus: Research findings, impact stories, year-in-review, institutional case studies, thought leadership on 2027 trends
- Product highlight: Play & Fair labs, Perpetual labs, research outputs, PPCS evidence
- Campaigns: Institutional buyers, foundations, learning designers, thought leadership positioning

## Monthly Planning Cadence

### Content Calendar Rhythm (Repeating)

**1st Monday of month:** Editorial team (Payton, Jamie, Lamis) meets to plan month's content themes, assign writers, identify repurposing opportunities.

**1st week:** Identify 2–3 anchor pieces:
- 1 long-form piece (Substack essay, research brief, case study) — 1500–2500 words
- 1–2 medium pieces (blog post, LinkedIn article, whirlpool session prep) — 800–1200 words
- 3–5 short-form pieces (tips, templates, graphics, video scripts) — < 200 words each

**2nd week:** Draft long-form piece; schedule social content; identify graphics/video opportunities.

**3rd week:** Publish long-form; distribute across channels; prepare email campaign; draft supporting social content.

**4th week:** Engagement push; reply to comments; capture testimonials or user-generated content; plan next month.

## Content Repurposing Pipeline

### One Piece → Six Channels (Example: PPCS Case Study)

**Original:** "How we helped [University] redesign [programme] for PRME compliance using PPCS" (Substack essay, 2000 words)

**Repurposing:**

1. **LinkedIn**
   - Article post (native LinkedIn version, 800 words, link to full Substack)
   - 3–4 supporting posts: pull key insights, data points, testimonials, methodology snippets
   - Cadence: publish article mid-week, supporting posts over next 2 weeks
   - Tone: authoritative, evidence-led, practitioner-focused

2. **Email**
   - Featured item in weekly digest (summary + CTA to full essay)
   - Segmented campaign to higher ed faculty (different subject line than learning designers)
   - CTAs: "Read the full case study" + "Sign up for PPCS webinar"

3. **Substack**
   - Full essay (published Thursday 6pm PT)
   - Includes embedded data visualisations (charts, graphs), sidebars (methodology, definitions), CTAs (whirlpool signup, Port CRM tracking)

4. **Instagram (if visual-friendly)**
   - "Before/after" graphic (course redesign visual)
   - 2–3 Reels: "3 lessons from this university's redesign journey" (15–30 sec each)
   - Carousel post with 5 key quotes from the case study
   - Tone: relatable, concrete, behind-the-scenes

5. **Bluesky**
   - Thread: "We just published a case study on [University]'s PPCS journey. Here's what we learned..." (3–5 posts unravelling key findings)
   - Engagement: reply to replies, link to full essay
   - Tone: conversational, idea-sharing, curious

6. **Conferences & Speaking**
   - Basis for breakout session proposal or panel: "Designing for PRME Compliance: 3 universities, 3 models"
   - Data/findings become presentation slides
   - Case study becomes speaker narrative
   - Attendees directed to Port CRM + Substack signup via QR code in slides

**Timeline:** Substack publish Thu → LinkedIn article Fri → Email Tue (following week) → Supporting social posts Wed–Fri → Conference talks sourced over Q (4–6 week lead time for CFPs).

## Weekly Execution Rhythm

### Monday (Asia–Pacific Audience Peak)
- Whirlpool with Press Play (4pm UTC / 9am PT)
- Instagram: 1 Reels post (high reach window)
- Bluesky: 2–3 topical posts

### Tuesday (High Engagement Across Channels)
- Team syncs (Lamis, Maria, Randall)
- Email: weekly digest curated (send Wed 9am PT)
- LinkedIn: 1 article or 2 short posts
- Content review & scheduling for rest of week

### Wednesday (Email Publish Day)
- Email: weekly digest ships 9am PT
- LinkedIn: supporting article or 2 posts published
- Substack: if essay week, prep final proofs

### Thursday (Substack & Weekend Planning)
- Substack: essay publishes 6pm PT (if 2/month cadence, alternating weeks)
- Email: monitor open rates, clicks
- Instagram: 1–2 posts scheduled for weekend (family engagement peak)
- Content planning call: next week's themes

### Friday (Engagement & Content Capture)
- Reply to all comments (LinkedIn, Instagram, Bluesky, Substack)
- Capture user-generated content, testimonials
- Document wins, metrics for weekly CFO/CMO reviews
- Instagram: 1–2 posts (weekend continuing)

### Saturday–Sunday (Audience-Specific Push)
- Instagram: Stories, behind-the-scenes, Reels (parent & teacher leisure time)
- Bluesky: lighter, playful threads (community building)
- Email: monitor engagement from mid-week sends

## Content Governance & Quality

**Editorial Approval Chain**
1. Writer (Jamie, Lamis, Garrett, Payton) drafts in Google Docs.
2. Peer review (second pair of eyes) — feedback 48 hours.
3. Brand voice check (Payton): lowercase, no buzzwords, British spelling, substantive.
4. Final approval (Garrett for strategy, Payton for execution) — 24 hours.
5. Schedule & publish (Payton).

**Tone Consistency**
- LinkedIn: authoritative, evidence-led, substantive
- Instagram: warm, relatable, permission-giving
- Bluesky: conversational, idea-sharing, curious
- Substack: narrative, personal, honest about trade-offs
- Email: warm, practical, clear CTAs
- Conferences: expert but accessible, interactive

**Data & Attribution**
- All inbound inquiries tagged in Port CRM with source (LinkedIn, email, conference, direct, etc.)
- Monthly review: which channels drive the most qualified leads?
- Quarterly: which content types resonate by segment?
- Annual: ROI per channel, per content type, per campaign.

## Handoff & Continuity

At end of each quarter, editorial team produces:
1. **What Worked:** top 3 content pieces, top 2 channels, top segment by lead quality.
2. **What Didn't:** lowest-engagement content, channels to reduce cadence.
3. **Next Quarter Adjustments:** theme priorities, channel mix, content types to amplify.
4. **Gaps:** audience segments under-served, competitor moves to respond to.

Memory updated in `.brain/memory/marketing/` for next quarter's planning.
