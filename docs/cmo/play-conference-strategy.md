## the one-line version

we are not "planning a conference." we are reverse-engineering a movement.
a full-scale in-person play event in new york in **october 2027** is the
destination. an online "mini" webinar in **october 2026** is the prototype.
a monthly rhythm of substack play sessions between now and then is how we
build the audience, the evidence, and the muscle to pull it off.

the whole thing is, in garrett's words from the call, "a giant marketing
campaign for us at some level." so it lives here, in Mo's brain, and payton
owns the campaign.

## why this matters strategically

three reasons this is worth the team's energy, not just a nice idea:

1. **it converts the network into a moat.** the peer network in
   `peer-network.md` (lightbulb, press play, care for education, education
   for sharing, six bricks, the lego foundation orbit) has been a list of
   warm relationships. this turns the list into a *convening role* — w.v
   becomes one of three orgs at the centre of a field-wide gathering. that
   is a positioning win no competitor in `competitive.md` can copy.

2. **it is post-lego-money infrastructure.** the explicit thesis from the
   call: build the thing that exists "after lego has left… learning to play
   under one arm and not a bag under the other arm." we stop waiting for
   foundation money and instead create the table everyone wants a seat at.

3. **it productises play.** the conference work has a service ladder behind
   it — **spark** (light play injection, ~$15k entry), **lead** (workshops
   and gatherings), **build** (soup-to-nuts conference management). every
   playdate, webinar, and the 2027 event is a top-of-funnel demo for that
   ladder. this is pipeline, not just brand.

## the positioning (the message we never break)

the through-line from the call, in plain language:

- **play is the vehicle, not the recess.** sarah: "it's not about bells and
  whistles being added on to the content; it's about driving the content
  *through* a playful approach." adults and kids both have "a radar for fake
  play." we never position play as the break between the real content. play
  *is* the content delivery.
- **we design the monday after.** the landing-page hook payton built:
  "90% of conference content is forgotten within a week. we design the
  in-between to stick." tagline in play: *"we're the ones thinking about the
  monday after."*
- **show, don't tell — as a rule, not a slogan.** if you present at anything
  we run, you *show* something, you don't talk at people. that is the floor
  for every contributor, including us. it applies to the website too
  (interactive, "see yourself in it") and to the webinar.
- **a new third thing.** not a conference, not an unconference. the call
  played with "conferment" / "ferment." lamis's frame is the **yoga retreat**:
  transformational, well-marketed, threshold-crossing. naming is w.v's job —
  it's a known strength.
- **enablers, not gurus.** the buyer (csr departments, corporates,
  governments, foundations, ecd and higher-ed) often "wants to change the
  world and isn't good at it." we help them "get crisp on their goals." every
  engagement is goal-based with pre/post measurement (lisa and sarah hold
  this line).

## the runway, reverse-engineered (2027 ← now)

### destination — october 2027 · in-person · new york

- working hypothetical: **~300 people, nyc, october 2027, venue tbd** (lisa
  is the venue expert). scale is deliberately undecided — could be 50 thought
  leaders in a "weird, small, funky venue" or a numbers play for csr
  representation. we hold both until the network mapping tells us which.
- model: an **integrated full experience** — "from the time you get your
  badge until you roll your bag out," nothing siloed. no recess play.
- convening: the **three orgs are the inner circle**; **~12–20 host orgs**
  bring their networks; lego education/foundation are *participants, not the
  centre*.
- stake in the ground (sarah): "we're going to do a thing in october 2027.
  even if it's the 10 of us… and if it's 500 people, it's 500 people." the
  commitment is fixed; the size is not.

### prototype — october 2026 · online · 2–3 hours

- a **"mini" webinar / online mini-conference**, ~2–3 hours, possibly run
  **twice for global time zones**.
- audience target: invite **20–50 of our closest friends**, campaign toward
  **~100 participants**.
- design principle: **co-create around a concept, not from scratch.** sarah's
  real fear — starting from a blank page "replicates the conversations that
  have been happening for three years." so *we* bring the frame and the "why";
  invitees bring the "what." a pre-meeting of ~12 sets the frame; the 30–50
  "plus ones" react and add colour.
- the "why" we open with (garrett): "how do we enable you? how do we use this
  space for you to ask for what you need and connect you with what you need." —
  i.e. get the field to *play together* instead of staying in isolated tribes.
- success = people "see themselves in this space" and see what 2027 becomes.
  every minute is showing, not telling.

### the runway cadence — now → oct 2026 · monthly play on substack

- lamis's **"communal playdates"**: accessible ~30-minute drop-in sessions,
  monthly, each built on a substack theme — "come together, focus on a theme,
  embody what it's like to use play to explore it." low stakes: "if three
  people show up but we get to play, that's amazing."
- mechanism: **substack first friday** (or set cadence). jamie's essays supply
  the theme (e.g. last week's "creasing, folding, unfolding" / origami);
  lamis hosts the live playdate off the back of it; payton cuts the clips.
- **every drop must tie to the october 2027 arc.** the closing logic from the
  call: "if we want to learn things between now and october 2026, we try them
  out in substacks and webinars and posts." the playdates are how we
  rehearse, gather evidence, and warm the invite list in public.
- **co-creator hook:** invite network people to *write for or be interviewed
  on* the substack. sarah: "people love to be part of making the thing… 'i was
  interviewed by lamis for this play conference thing'" — and then they share
  it to their own network. each interview is both content and an invitation.
