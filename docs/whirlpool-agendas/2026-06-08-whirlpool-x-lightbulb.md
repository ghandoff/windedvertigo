# whirlpool x lightbulb learning lab — 8 june 2026

> 9:00–10:30am PT · 90 minutes
> attendees: garrett, maria, payton, jamie, lamis + sarah wolman, lisa prince (+ possibly lizzie)
> format: playful, experiential, collaborative — not a pitch meeting

---

## the thread (2 min)

open with this: a little over a year ago, sarah and lisa hosted the inaugural in-person whirlpool in nicasio. some of us were there — garrett, payton, lamis — and some of us weren't, but what came out of that retreat has shaped everything since. carly pressed record and the team prototyped a podcast on the spot — origami spinning tops, alan watts, "what does birth mean to you?" — and nothing about it was planned, and all of it was real.

since then the team has grown. maria has become the heart of w.v's learning design. aaron (fruit) builds the visual world everything lives in. the work that started in nicasio didn't stay in nicasio — it became the harbour, the webinars, the apps, the way we whirlpool. sarah and lisa planted a seed, and the people in this room (whether or not they were in that room) are what grew from it.

today we're back together to figure out what we can build for each other — not someday, now.

---

## 1. one-year polaroid (10 min)

quick round: each person shares one thing that's changed about their work in the last year — one sentence, no slides. not "what happened at nicasio" but "where are you now?" this lets everyone contribute equally, whether they were at the retreat or joined the story later.

the constraint keeps it tight and reveals where people's energy is now.

**what to listen for:** where lightbulb's current work overlaps with w.v's, and vice versa. note these on a shared board (miro or just a doc).

---

## 2. show & tell — two-way (20 min total: 10 min each side)

this isn't a demo — it's "show me your favourite thing you've made recently."

**w.v shows (10 min):**
- the harbour — 19+ interactive learning tools, live since may 28. pick 2-3 to actually play for 60 seconds each (values auction? lines become loops? co.rubric companion?)
- the PEDAL conference simulator — the "tell to show" bridge that lets clients experience a reimagined conference in 90 seconds

**lightbulb shows (10 min):**
- the lightbulb way — their emerging methodology (show don't tell + belonging/inclusion + design sprints + application). sarah and lisa tested this at creative mornings. what did they learn?
- whatever they're proudest of from the last year

**ground rule:** no explaining what it is first. show it, then talk about it. (this is literally sarah's philosophy — "show me vs tell me.")

## 3. the conferences page — what collaboration already looks like (10 min)

pull up [windedvertigo.com/conferences](https://windedvertigo.com/conferences) and walk sarah and lisa through it live. this is the w.v x press play offering that payton has been building — conference redesign, interventions, the PEDAL simulator as a sales tool.

the reason to show this now: it's proof that w.v is already building collaborative service packages with partners. the natural question for sarah and lisa becomes "what would a w.v x lightbulb version of this look like?" — or even "what if lightbulb was part of this offering?"

let the conversation breathe here. sarah's facilitation expertise + lisa's belonging/inclusion work + w.v's interactive tools + press play's conference infrastructure = something none of them could pitch alone.

## 5. the first swap (15 min)

sarah said something on may 8 that should be the operating principle today: "lower the bar for engagement — don't wait for big funding milestones to collaborate."

so: what's one thing each org can do for the other in the next two weeks, no contract, no scope document?

**starter ideas from the meeting notes:**

- w.v builds lightbulb a simple linkedin content plan + first 3 posts (payton leads, garrett reviews) — this is literally what sarah asked for
- lightbulb reviews w.v's conference pitch (the press play collab) with fresh eyes and connects us to one warm lead from their network
- garrett sends sarah those 5-6 testimonial bullet points she asked for on may 28 (was this done?)
- w.v prototypes a game-like post-engagement check-in tool for lightbulb's next client (this came up may 8 — sarah was excited about it)
- lightbulb gives w.v feedback on 2-3 harbour tools from a facilitator's perspective (what would make these useful in a workshop?)

pick two. one from each direction. put names and dates on them before the call ends.

## 6. the shape of this (8 min)

what does the ongoing relationship look like? options that came up in past calls:

- **co-branding**: "winded.vertigo x lightbulb learning lab" on shared offerings (may 28 discussion)
- **mutual promotion**: each org promotes the other's work — "easier to promote others than self" (sarah, may 28)
- **standing check-in**: monthly or bi-weekly call to keep momentum (not just when there's a project)
- **shared convening**: the 30-person learning-through-play event as a north star
- **guest whirlpools**: sarah and/or lisa join a w.v whirlpool periodically (this was an action item from may 8)

don't overcommit. name the next touchpoint and one shared artifact (a doc, a miro board, a slack channel) where the collaboration lives between calls.

---

## 7. close — the nicasio question (5 min)

end where it started. the retreat's prompt was "what does birth mean to you?" — and the answers were about transformation, new beginnings, the clarity that comes after.

new prompt for the close: **"what's trying to be born between us?"**

one sentence each. no pressure to be deep. but if something lands, write it down.
