# amna inception package v1 — critique & what to bring into v2

_cARL · 2026-06-25 · internal_
_a critical read of the parallel cowork conversation's "Amna at 10 — Inception Package (v1)", and the merge plan for our version two_

---

## verdict in one paragraph

v1 is strong — noticeably more developed than our plan as an *operational inception spine*. It did the single most important thing we hadn't: it actually read Amna's **strategic spine documents** and reconstructed Amna's own theory of practice, so the synthesis is positioned to "honour, test, and extend" a theory Amna already articulated rather than invent one. It also has a sharper field-positioning argument, a designed evidence-and-claims map, a premortem, grouped kick-off questions, and a reading sprint. **But** it has one glaring blind spot — **data governance and security is almost entirely absent** — and one structural risk: **it pre-writes its conclusions before reading the evidence**, leaning into an advocacy frame the budget/independence can't fully cash. Our job for v2 is to fuse v1's substance and operational detail with our data-governance rigour, contractual realism, and a more disciplined evidence-humility posture.

One correction to our own work: v1 was **right about the inventory and we were incomplete** — the **"Amna Strategies and processes reports" folder (15 docs) is in our share** (verified today); our first inventory pass missed it. The Evidence folder has **three** top-level corpora, not two.

## what v1 does better than our plan — bring these into v2

1. **It read Amna's own strategy, not just the evaluations.** v1 mined 15 spine docs (Strategy 2024-2028; *Healing Ecosystems — Strategic Evolution*; *Internal Narrative 2026*; *MEL Strategic Enabler / TOC*; *Cumulative Reach*; *Direct & Indirect Reach*; *2025 Evaluation Level Outcomes*) and extracted Amna's self-theory:
   - the **L1–L4 ecosystem architecture** (community healing → master trainers/fellows → regional healing ecosystems → global healing network);
   - the **Enter → Build → Sustain → Embed** flow mapped to pillars (Emergency Response → Community Partnerships/Baytna/Dinami → Global Healing Network → Systems of Care);
   - Amna's **named active ingredients** and the **eight outcome domains** (safety, regulation, belonging, joy, agency, wellbeing, protective relationships, sense of future).
   **→ v2 must read the spine folder first and centre Amna's own framework.** This is the biggest single upgrade. The stance "the synthesis doesn't construct Amna's ToC — it honours, tests, and extends it" is correct and we should adopt it (with the independence caveat below).

2. **Five "spine themes" as analytical anchors** — identity-informed (not trauma-alone); the **cascade-vs-relational-depth tension**; implicit→explicit systems work; **what outlasts the grant** (capacity/culture/relationships/workforce); **honest limitation-naming as a cultural signature**. These are genuinely useful, Amna-specific hooks. Adopt them — but as *hypotheses to test*, not settled themes (see critique #2).

3. **A sharper field-positioning argument.** Not "Amna is alternative/complementary" but "**Amna is the decade-long operational implementation of where the empirical evidence and the decolonial critique are both converging**," plus two concrete **evidence gaps Amna could credibly claim to close**: (a) *accompaniment* has never been isolated as an active ingredient in a humanitarian MHPSS trial; (b) *communities of practice as a healing mechanism for displaced people themselves* is unevidenced. This is a fundable, distinctive framing and seeds the Future Research Agenda. Adopt — held provisionally until the evidence supports it.

4. **The Initial Programme Theory (IPT) as a distinct artefact**, written *before* the inception note: 6–12 candidate CMO conjectures in explicit "in context C, mechanism M fires reasoning/resource R, producing outcome O" form, each tagged **tacit / documented / contested**. This is more concrete than our CMO treatment. Adopt the artefact and the template.

5. **A designed Evidence & Claims Map.** Bubble grid (pillars × outcome domains) where each cell carries a **confidence rating** (●●● well-supported → ○○○ gap), **bubble size = evidence count**, **colour = source mix** (external eval / practitioner narrative / MEAL data / training feedback), plus a **source-mix transparency strip** to pre-empt "where's your RCT?". Paired with a CERQual Summary-of-Findings table with explicit **"safe claim language" vs "language to avoid"** columns. Ours described the confidence framework; theirs designed the artefact. Adopt the design.

6. **A premortem table** (failure mode → pre-committed inception-stage safeguard), including a **rival-theory test** for each CMO, **context-stratified reporting** ("where this doesn't hold"), a **partnership-overload cap**, and the **"another evaluation" trap**. Richer than our risks list. Adopt.

7. **Grouped kick-off questions for Amna** (evidence portfolio; scope/emphasis; the open questions Amna itself names; voice/authorship/validation; operational) — far more developed than our five, and named to the right people (Hejer as MEL lead). Adopt and merge with our five.

8. **A tiered reading list with file paths + a first-30-days sequence + reading-hour budget.** Operationally excellent. Adopt.

9. **A validation cadence** (kick-off → IPT validation → mid-synthesis → draft review) and the principle "**Amna holds veto on representation of its model; we hold integrity on findings**." Adopt the cadence; sharpen the principle (critique #4).

10. **Anti-extractive epistemological commitments** (community-language register; first-pass coding from Amna's own logs; provenance-tracked claims; interpretation log; surface-tensions/refuse-premature-resolution; return points; refusal protocol; citation justice). These align with our own Research Governance Suite's "anti-extractive practice" value. Adopt selectively, balanced against independence.

## where v1 is weak or risky — fix in v2

1. **Data governance and security is almost absent — our biggest differentiator.** v1 processes **~3,900 files including child photos, named case studies, and interview transcripts**, yet says almost nothing about: where identifiable special-category data is stored, the fact that **Exhibit 1 approves our Google Workspace only for *anonymised/pseudonymised* material**, EU data residency, access-locking to the four named team, ZDR mode for AI processing, the required **AI-use declaration** to Amna, or retention/deletion at close. Its premortem has no data-security row. **v2 must carry our plan's §5 wholesale** — this is precisely the Google Drive question Garrett raised, and it's a credibility and compliance gate, not a footnote.

2. **It pre-writes its conclusions before reading the evidence — confirmation-bias risk baked in.** v1 already asserts Amna's five spine themes, the "Amna is what the field has been arguing for" thesis, and the two evidence-gap claims — all drawn from **Amna's own strategic self-presentation plus background literature, before the evaluation findings are read.** Ironically it names "ideological capture" as failure mode #2 while partly committing it. The synthesis's value to funders depends on **independence**; if it reads as a sympathetic insider narrative, sector peers discount it. **v2 fix:** hold every one of v1's themes and positioning claims as **hypotheses to be tested against the evidence**, apply the rival-theory test to *Amna's own narrative* (not just to CMOs), and keep the "Amna is what the field wanted" line out of the brief until the evidence earns it.

3. **It is over-scoped for £20k / ~52 team-days.** v1 stacks a full 3ie/Campbell EGM + realist CMO layer + CERQual SoF + (implied) contribution analysis + eight anti-extractive commitments with interpretation logs + rival-theory tests + context-stratified reporting + a citation-justice hand-search. That is a lot of apparatus for a desk review with ~52 days across four people (week 1 alone is budgeted at 25–30 reading hours). Risk: **methodology theatre we can't fully deliver**, or a blown timeline. **v2 fix:** prioritise — the EGM + CERQual claim discipline + IPT/CMO are core; the full anti-extractive apparatus and citation-justice hand-search should be scoped to what the budget bears, with the rest named as aspirational.

4. **The anti-extractive framing + "Amna veto on representation" can compromise independence and sit in tension with the TOR.** The TOR explicitly wants a transparent examination of "**where Amna struggles, where outcomes vary, and why**" and "key limitations and risks." Combine a pre-leaning narrative, eight decolonial commitments, and Amna holding representational veto, and the deep learning synthesis risks losing its critical edge. **v2 fix:** keep the anti-extractive commitments for *how we handle community voice and data*, but draw a brighter line: Amna validates factual accuracy and safeguarding; **winded.vertigo retains analytical independence over findings, including unflattering ones.** That's also what makes the report useful to Amna's own strategy.

5. **Some specifics need verifying, not trusting.** v1 names evaluators and artefacts our pass didn't confirm — "360 Consulting Group" (Baytna Lebanon), "Jon Korfmacher" (Ukraine), "IPA Baytna materials," a precise **3,923**-file count, and the Yousafzai/Zanial Harvard authorship. Several check out (Harvard SPH, UVA, Chapin Hall, Nexus all align; the Strategies folder is confirmed). But treat the un-verified specifics as **to-confirm during the reading sprint**, not as established fact in a client-facing document.

6. **Deliverable sequencing contradicts best practice.** v1's workplan puts the **Impact Brief at ~week 8, before the Deep Learning Synthesis at ~week 13.** Best practice (and our plan) is to **write the rigorous internal synthesis first, then derive the public brief from it** — otherwise the brief's claims aren't yet backed. (The TOR's *calendar* happens to put the brief first, but the contract re-based the timeline to September, so we can sequence properly.) **v2 fix:** synthesis-first, brief-derived; reconcile with Amna at kick-off.

7. **It under-engages the contract's commercial reality.** v1 references £20k and "September" but not the **30/30/40 payment gates tied to approval**, the **two-revision-round cap**, the **key-personnel lock**, or the **IP/publication terms**. These shape what we commit to. **v2 fix:** fold in our plan's §2 contract-adjustments section.

8. **Timeline still assumes the old rhythm.** It anchors to "~2 weeks from project start" and a 13-week run without flagging that the milestone dates are **superseded and must be formally re-baselined to the 30 Sep long-stop** with Amna. Carry our explicit re-baseline action.
