## 4. methodology — the approach we'll run

Aligned to our proposal and to current best practice (realist & MHPSS-synthesis literature; see cARL library + the two methodology digests behind this plan).

1. **structured desk review** of a defined corpus — build a document register (type, date, geography, pillar, independence, provenance) and a parallel "missing-evidence" log; appraise each source on provenance/credibility, methodological strength, and relevance (Scott; Gough weight-of-evidence). Double-screen a sample for reliability.
2. **thematic synthesis** (Thomas & Harden) — line-by-line → descriptive themes → analytical themes, with a clear audit trail.
3. **realist-informed analysis** (Pawson & Tilley; RAMESES standards; Dalkin on mechanisms) — express findings as **context–mechanism–outcome (CMO) configurations**; articulate 8–15 initial CMOs as the programme theory in the inception note, then confirm/refute/refine against the evidence. Where a mechanism is plausible but under-evidenced, label it "**working theory — requires validation**."
4. **contribution analysis** (Mayne) — for each headline impact claim, build a contribution story that names rival/co-contributing factors (other agencies, host systems, funding cycles) rather than overclaiming attribution in a multi-actor ecosystem.
5. **evidence & gap map** — internal EGM: rows = pillars / IASC pyramid layers, columns = outcome levels & domains; cells coloured by confidence. This is the centrepiece visual and doubles as the future-research-agenda tool.
6. **evidence-confidence framework** (GRADE-CERQual) — rate every finding on methodological limitations, coherence, adequacy, relevance → **strong / emerging / gap**, recorded in a Summary-of-Findings table, with a **fixed claim-language convention** (strong = "the evidence demonstrates"; emerging = "evidence suggests, though limited"; gap = "insufficient to claim — priority for primary collection"). Verbs never outrun the band — this is our main defence against overclaiming.

**MHPSS field framing (to land with Amna's stakeholders and funders):**

- map outcomes to the **IASC Common M&E Framework** taxonomy (functioning, subjective well-being, distress, coping, social behaviour, social connectedness) and locate Amna honestly on the **IASC intervention pyramid** (layers 2–3: community/family supports and focused non-specialised support).
- foreground the **"support the supporter / relational quality is the active ingredient"** through-line that runs across Amna's model — caregiver→child (Baytna/ECD), and accompaniment + supervision + communities of practice → partner capability (GHN, Community Partnerships). Frame capacity-building by **competency and supervision**, not training-event counts.
- **centre community-held definitions of healing and lived-experience/practitioner knowledge as primary evidence** (decolonial/locally-led critique), and be transparent where the measurement framework is imported. This is also the honest answer to the eval-rubric concern that our team is MHPSS-adjacent: our value-add is rigorous **evidence synthesis**, not clinical practice.

## 5. data governance, ethics & safeguarding (incl. the Google Drive question)

The evidence is **special-category personal data** — child photos and free-play portfolios, named case studies, beneficiary databases, salary scans, and Arabic/English interview transcripts. Amna is controller; we are processor (Annex C). This section is load-bearing, not boilerplate.

**On storing this in Google Drive — the honest read.** Google Workspace (winded.vertigo org account) *is* a contractually approved platform — **but Exhibit 1 approves it specifically for "anonymised or pseudonymised material,"** while Amna's own infrastructure (item 4) is the approved home for direct access to identifiable material. Right now the **raw, identifiable, special-category corpus is sitting in our Workspace shared drive**, which is a scope mismatch worth closing. Specific gaps and fixes:

- **scope:** treat the raw Evidence folder as a **read-only source we access**, and do our actual working/analysis on **anonymised or pseudonymised extracts** in our Workspace and in the EU-hosted Supabase "data spine" (Exhibit 1 item 3). Ideally identifiable raw data is accessed from **Amna's own infrastructure** (item 4) rather than copied into ours — confirm with Amna which they prefer.
- **data residency:** Annex C requires no processing outside the UK/EEA except approved platforms. Standard Google Workspace does not guarantee EEA-only storage at rest unless **Data Regions / Assured Controls** are configured (Business Plus/Enterprise tiers). **Action: confirm our Workspace tier and set an EU data region, or document Drive as an approved exception.**
- **access control:** restrict the Evidence shared drive to the **four named team members only**, enforce MFA, disable link-sharing/"anyone with the link," and turn off broad shared-drive inheritance. (During discovery we also saw unrelated evidence folders under a **personal gmail account** — keep all Amna data strictly on the org account, never personal.)
- **local sync:** the folder is syncing to at least one local Mac via Drive CloudStorage — minimise local copies, ensure full-disk encryption (FileVault), and avoid downloads of the sensitive sub-folders.
- **AI use:** Claude is an approved tool (Exhibit 1 item 2) **with ZDR mode for any identifiable/sensitive/safeguarding material, no-training, and named-team human review.** We must file an **AI-use declaration** with Amna (tool, purpose, data, safeguards, review process) at inception, and never input identifiable data into non-approved tools (e.g. web search). This planning work has stayed at metadata level for exactly this reason.
- **retention/deletion:** plan now for return/secure-deletion of all data at project close (Annex C 2.x), and keep a processing record.
- **governance leads:** per our Research Governance Suite, assign and document at inception the **Ethics Lead, Data Protection Lead, Data Quality Lead, and Safeguarding Lead** — and apply a do-no-harm disclosure filter so **no participant/child is identifiable in any public deliverable**.

> bottom line on Drive: not a crisis, but not yet right. The org-account Workspace is approved in principle; the gap is that identifiable special-category data is currently in a general-purpose drive rather than an access-locked, EU-region, anonymised-extracts-only setup. Close the four fixes above (access lock, EU region, raw-vs-extract separation, retention plan) and confirm the arrangement with Amna as controller. This is a governance judgement, not legal advice — worth a quick confirmation with Amna's DPO.

The **inception note** is deliverable B, the paid 30% gate, and a commitment device: it locks scope, framework, and claim discipline *before* analysis so findings can't be quietly reshaped. It must contain:

- purpose, review questions, and explicit out-of-scope statements (mapped to TOR §4 A–E).

- **evidence portfolio profiling** — the document register / first-pass evidence map (volume, type, coverage by pillar/geography/year) plus the missing-evidence log.
- **initial programme theory** — the candidate CMO configurations to be tested, organised by pillar.
- **methods** — cataloguing rubric, appraisal tools, coding approach, EGM framework, and the **CERQual confidence framework + pre-committed claim-language convention**.
- **workplan, milestones, roles, QA gates**, and the re-baselined timeline to 30 Sep.
- **validation cadence** — at least two sense-check points (emerging-findings + pre-final), explicitly including **practitioner and lived-experience voice**, and how disagreements are logged.
- **ethics & safeguarding protocol** — the data-handling, anonymisation, and disclosure controls from §5; the four named governance leads; the AI-use declaration.
