Hi everyone,

It's been too long since we were all in the same (virtual) room, and Maria and I have been talking about why that should change — soon. A warm welcome to Jan and Casper (Press Play) and Sarah and Lisa (Lightbulb Learning Lab), who we're delighted to bring into this conversation for the first time; and to Michael and Brent (Care for Education), Dina, Edgardo and Gabi (Education for Sharing), and Fernande (History Co:Lab) — you already know the shape of what we've built together.

Here's the idea we'd like to put in front of you. Individually, each of our organisations does remarkable work in play, learning and human development. But the opportunities that could most change what we're each able to do — the larger grants and commissions — increasingly ask for a scale, a breadth of credentials, and a track record that none of us carries alone. Together, we plainly do.

So we'd like to reconvene this group with a concrete purpose: to turn our Learning Through Play network into a working consortium — a way to qualify for and respond to those bigger opportunities as one, while each of us stays entirely ourselves.

The heart of it is a simple exchange. We pool what we each bring — our people, our evidence, our distinctive strengths — into a shared, living picture of the group's collective capability. Where one of us has a gap, another fills it; where one of us is reaching for something we couldn't credibly chase alone, the group's combined expertise makes it possible. And winded.vertigo would put the systems we've been building — to map capabilities, surface evidence, and assemble strong proposals quickly — to work for the whole consortium, not just ourselves.
