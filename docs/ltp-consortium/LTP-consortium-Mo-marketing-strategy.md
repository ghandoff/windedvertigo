# Learning Through Play Consortium — Marketing Strategy (Mo)
_Outward marketing for the consortium as a recognisable entity — not just bid-response. Prepared 2026-06-30. Companion to the consortium-bidding brief + the Garrett×Maria transcript._

## 1. The job to be done
Make the consortium a thing funders and primes **seek out** — a recognised name in play-based learning and human development that signals scale, credibility, and the ability to deliver across contexts. Marketing is not a brochure; it's the credibility engine that makes the bidding work easier.

## 2. Positioning
**One-liner (working):** _"A consortium of play, learning and human-development organisations that qualify, design and deliver as one — so funders get the breadth of a network with the accountability of a team."_

**Proof points** (what makes it believable):
- A pooled, evidence-backed **capability map** (the shared canonical-CV + knowledge graph) — visible proof of collective expertise and track record.
- Named, complementary members spanning geographies and specialisms (early childhood, history/civic learning, values education, play design, etc.).
- A precedent the sector already trusts — consortia like **PlayMatters** (IRC + Plan + War Child, LEGO Foundation) show this model wins and delivers.

**What we are NOT:** a merger, a re-brand of any member, or a marketing front. Each org stays itself; the consortium is the shared surface.

## 3. Identity (for the group to choose)
Keep "Learning Through Play" in the name for continuity, recognition and search. Directions:
- **Learning Through Play Consortium (LTPC)** — clearest, most literal, fundable-sounding.
- **The Play Collective** — warmer, brandable, less self-explanatory.
- **Playful Futures** — aspirational, broader than "play" alone.
Recommendation: lead with a literal name for funders (LTPC) and allow a warmer public handle. A simple wordmark + a "member of" badge each org can display.

## 4. Audiences (in priority order)
1. **Foundations & funders** in play/learning (LEGO Foundation, education foundations, family-planning/SRH funders adjacent to members' work).
2. **INGO primes & multilaterals** that sub-contract design/MEL/curriculum (IRC, Plan, UNICEF, IDB) — the consortium as a credible teaming partner.
3. **Government / ministry** procurement where a network's breadth clears credential bars none of us meet alone.
4. **Peer networks & convenings** (the warm ecosystem that feeds referrals and members).

## 5. Channels
- **Public web presence:** a lightweight consortium page + a **public face of the knowledge graph / capability map** — an interactive "what this network can do and has done" surface. This is the single highest-leverage asset; it doubles as proof and as content.
- **LinkedIn:** a consortium presence plus a **co-posting protocol** — each launch/win amplified by all members from their own handles (reach multiplies without anyone losing their brand).
- **Capability one-pager + short deck:** the leave-behind for funder/prime conversations.
- **Sector convenings & speaking:** position members as the consortium's voice at play/learning events.
- **Warm-intro engine:** the members' combined networks are the best top-of-funnel — make introductions a deliberate, tracked motion.

## 6. Co-marketing model (no brand dilution)
- Shared wordmark + "member of the Learning Through Play Consortium" badge on member sites.
- Attribution rule: consortium posts always credit the member(s) who did the work (mirrors the CV-integrity rule — never absorb a member's work into a faceless "we").
- Cross-post protocol: consortium publishes → members amplify within 48h from their own channels.
- Each member keeps full ownership of its own brand, content and clients; consortium marketing only ever *adds*.

## 7. The knowledge graph as a marketing asset
The shared canonical-CV/knowledge-graph isn't just internal plumbing — made partly public, it's a **credibility engine and a content engine**: capability stories, evidence maps, "who's done what where" — the kind of proof funders rarely see laid out clearly. It's also the most defensible differentiator vs. an ad-hoc teaming arrangement.

## 8. Content engine (low-effort, high-trust)
- **Capability stories:** short, evidence-led pieces on what the network can do (drawn from the graph).
- **Thought leadership:** pooled expertise on play-based learning, measurement, and human development — punches above any single member's weight.
- **Win/announcement cadence:** every joint pursuit and award becomes a co-marketed moment.

## 9. First 90 days
- **Weeks 1–2:** group agrees name + identity; w.v stands up a one-page wordmark + holding page.
- **Weeks 3–4:** capability one-pager + the first public slice of the knowledge-graph/evidence map.
- **Weeks 5–6:** coordinated LinkedIn launch (all members co-post); "member of" badges live.
- **Weeks 7–9:** 2–3 targeted funder/prime touchpoints using the deck; first thought-leadership piece.
- **Weeks 10–12:** first co-marketed pipeline moment (a joint pursuit announced or a capability story tied to a live call).

## 10. Metrics
- **Awareness:** consortium page traffic, LinkedIn reach, inbound enquiries.
- **Pipeline:** joint opportunities sourced *because* of the consortium identity (warm intros, inbound).
- **Member engagement:** % of members co-posting, contributing to the graph, bringing opportunities.
